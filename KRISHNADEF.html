Summary Verdict

Krishna Defence sells the physical things Indian Navy warships used to import from Russia — bulb bars (naval hull sections), special alloy weld consumables for underwater platforms, ballast bricks for submarines, armoured steel for T-90 tank chassis. The moat is regulatory: naval-approved source status, DRDO/DGQA certifications, and Russia-derived qualification for critical chemistry. New entrants take years of trial and approval to qualify.

The FY26 print showed the operating leverage kicking in — revenue ₹245 Cr (+29%), PAT ₹38 Cr (+86%), OPM 22% (up from 15% FY24), debt down from ₹10 Cr to ₹2 Cr, and — most importantly — FCF flipped from −₹23 Cr (FY25) to +₹66 Cr (FY26) with CFO at 189% of operating profit. Working capital days halved from 110 to 53. Debtor days went from 199 (FY19) to 52 (FY26) — a genuine collections improvement, not a one-year artefact.

The stock is not cheap on trailing multiples (P/E 50, P/B 10). But peer defence names trade at P/E 60-90 (industry median 62 across 31 companies), and Krishna is one of the few with 30%+ organic growth, debt-free BS, 22% OPM, and 60% promoter. The bet is that FY27-28 delivery re-rates the earnings base, not that the multiple compresses.

The concern that has to be addressed head-on: promoter holding dropped from 73.4% (Sep 2022) to 60.0% (Mar 2026), a 13.4-percentage-point decline in 3 years. Most of the drop was concentrated in Mar-May 2024 (68.3% → 62.3%) — timing suggests fresh equity issuance during capacity buildout rather than open-market selling. Subsequent moves have been marginal (60.02% → 59.90% over Q1 2026). Not a red flag if the pattern doesn't continue; if promoter goes below 55%, exit.

Bull Case

Defence indigenization is India's most durable structural theme through 2030 — the government has budgeted for it, the Navy has publicly earmarked ~$1B for AUV alone, and the government is actively pushing PLI + strategic steel schemes that Krishna is aligned with (MoU with Ministry of Steel). Krishna's specific edge is that its products are the physical inputs — bulb bars, weld consumables, armour steel, ballast bricks — that the Navy used to import from Russia, and that supply chain is now permanently disrupted. Each certification (AS9100D aerospace, IRS commercial shipbuilding, Naval Approved Source) is a multi-year gate that the company has already cleared, and the market ahead is opening not closing.

The FY26 print showed the compounding engine turning on. Bulb bar capacity doubled in Apr 2025 (2,000-2,500 → 4,000-4,500 tons); operating leverage on that capacity is already visible in the 22% OPM print. Product mix is shifting toward higher-margin armour steel (17 → 26 variants supplied, "customer confidence" per management) and weld consumables. FCF turned strongly positive to ₹66 Cr, working capital collapsed from 110 to 53 days, and the company built a ₹65 Cr liquid reserve — this is the shape of a business preparing for the next capacity round without needing external funding.

The optionality on top of the base case is unusually rich. Aircraft arresting gear (design win, no revenue yet, NDAs cover the value), India's largest AUV under development with the Navy+DRDO (with $1B Navy earmark for the segment), and smart ammunition via Taharabadkar (₹25 Cr Navy grant to develop indigenous alternative to imported Leonardo system). None of these are in the base case guidance — management explicitly said FY27-28 projections do not include them. If even one delivers, the earnings base steps up beyond the 30% guidance.

Bear Case

The absolute valuation is not defensible if the growth engine slows. At P/E 50 and P/B 10, any print that fails to hit 30%+ growth would compress the multiple 30-40%. The Q3 FY26 miss (revenue actually softer than trend) was rescued by Q4 (₹65 Cr vs Q3 ₹64 Cr — flat sequentially) — if Q1 FY27 comes in below ₹60 Cr, the growth story becomes a story again, not a fact.

Customer concentration is high enough to bite: one shipyard is 35-40% of revenue, another 20%. Indian shipbuilding is dominated by 3-4 government yards (Cochin Shipyard, MDL, GRSE, Hindustan Shipyard) — losing a program on any one is a material event. Krishna has qualified with multiple yards, but the concentration risk is not diversified away.

The developmental optionality (AUV, smart weapons, arresting gear) is 2-3 years from revenue at minimum, per management. If the base case guidance slips because Operation-Sindoor-style disruptions recur (management flagged 4-5 month delays in FY26), or because shipyards continue placing smaller batch orders (as noted in the call), the earnings could grind flat for 2 quarters before recovery. At current valuation, 2 flat quarters is a -25% event.

The promoter dilution history — 73.4% → 59.9% in less than 3 years — is the single biggest governance mark. Most of it looks structural (equity raise during capacity buildout, IPO), not opportunistic selling, but the trend is unambiguous. If it continues past 55%, the alignment story is materially weaker.

Growth Trigger Scan (Kamayaka framework)

Multiple triggers firing simultaneously — this is the signal.

  • ✅ Capacity Expansion: Bulb bar capacity doubled Apr 2025. New 50,000 sq ft plot purchased for further expansion. CWIP visible on BS.
  • ✅ Operating Leverage: OPM 15% (FY24) → 22% (FY26) = 700 bps expansion on 132% revenue growth = classic operating leverage on doubled capacity.
  • ✅ Margin Expansion: Explicitly attributed to mix shift (armour + weld consumables higher margin than bulb bars) + productivity (automation, yield). Sustained across 3 quarters, not a one-print artefact.
  • ⚠️ Promoter Buying: No open-market promoter accumulation visible. FIIs and DIIs building small positions (0% → 1.36% / 0% → 1.32% over 12 months) — institutional interest is the substitute signal.
  • ✅ Regulatory Tailwind: Defence indigenization mandate, PLI on strategic steel, MoU with Ministry of Steel. Navy's ~$1B AUV earmark.
  • ⚠️ Turnaround: Not applicable — this is a growth story not a turnaround.

Four triggers active. Combined with capacity headroom to ₹400-500 Cr, that's the setup for an 18-24 month earnings re-rate.

Constraints: Domestic-only sales (steel products classified, no exports permitted). Raw material inflation acknowledged (fuel, steel, conversion cost) but impact "not substantial."
