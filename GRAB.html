Phase 1 — Business Overview

What Grab Does

Grab is Southeast Asia's leading superapp — a single platform integrating ride-hailing, food delivery, grocery, fintech (GrabFin), and enterprise services. Operates across 8 countries: Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Cambodia, Myanmar.

Three Segments:

  • Deliveries (food, grocery, mart): 51% of FY2024 revenue ($1.10B). GMV $8.3B. Take rate ~13.3%
  • Mobility (ride-hailing): 29% of FY2024 revenue ($0.63B). GMV $3.3B. Take rate 19.1%
  • Financial Services (GrabFin): 13% of FY2024 revenue ($0.28B) — fastest growing; digital banking (GXS Bank Singapore, GX Bank Malaysia), lending, insurance, payments

Revenue Model: Platform take rates on GMV + lending interest income + insurance premiums + enterprise B2B services

Phase 3 — Market Opportunity

TAM

  • SEA digital economy GMV: $300B in 2025 → $1T by 2030 (Google/Temasek/Bain e-Conomy SEA 2025)
  • SEA ride-hailing: $16B (2024) → $28B (2030), CAGR 10%
  • SEA food delivery: $20B → $37B, CAGR 11%
  • SEA digital financial services: $39B → $110B, CAGR 23% ← highest growth

Penetration Gap

  • Digital payments penetration SEA: 52% vs 90%+ in developed markets
  • 400M+ adults underbanked or unbanked across SEA — GrabFin's structural tailwind
  • Online food delivery: 15–20% penetration vs 35%+ in China/Korea

Why Grab Wins

Grab sits at the intersection of daily life (mobility + food) and financial services — generating the transaction data needed to underwrite credit where traditional banks can't. This is the flywheel: rides → food → payments → lending → insurance.

Phase 5 — Reinvestment Runway

GrabFin — The Re-rating Story

  • GXS Bank (Singapore, 2023): $1B loan book milestone reached FY2025
  • GX Bank (Malaysia, 2024): Fastest-growing digital bank in Malaysia
  • BNPL, insurance, wealth products — all early stage, high optionality
  • Grab processes $22.9B GMV/year — natural funnel for financial products
  • If GrabFin reaches 25% of revenue at higher margins → significant earnings upgrade

GoTo Merger

  • If approved: Grab gains dominant Indonesia market position (population 270M, SEA's largest economy)
  • Indonesia was Grab's weakest market due to GoTo competition → combined entity would be #1 across all 8 markets
  • Status: Under Indonesian KPPU (antitrust) review, decision expected H1 2026
