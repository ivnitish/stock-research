Why It's Interesting

  • Electron rocket is operational. 50+ launches, ~90% success rate. Only reliable small-satellite launch vehicle available commercially outside SpaceX.
  • Neutron rocket = the real bet. Medium-lift rocket targeting the reusable launch market. If Neutron works, RKLB becomes a genuine SpaceX competitor in the commercial launch market. Still in development, 2026-27 timeline.
  • Space Systems revenue. Beyond launches, RKLB makes spacecraft components and runs full satellite missions. This is recurring, contract-based revenue — more predictable than launch.
  • Government contracts. US DoD, NASA — RKLB has a track record of winning government contracts. Provides revenue floor.

Key Concerns

  • Pre-profit at the company level. Cash burn is ongoing.
  • Neutron is still unproven — development delays common in rockets.
  • SpaceX's Falcon 9 and Starship will always have cost advantages at scale.
  • $14B market cap on pre-profit business is pricing in a lot of success.
  • PE is not applicable — valued on revenue multiple and optionality.
