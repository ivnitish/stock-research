### 2026-04-15 — Patels Airtemp 33rd AGM | Sanjey Kumar Patel (CMD)

[Full Summary](channel/NA_EPMtzXum5o0_summary.md) · [Raw Transcript](channel/NA_EPMtzXum5o0.md) · [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EPMtzXum5o0)

**Key adds (non-obvious, specific):**
- **NPCIL is current revenue, not optionality:** CMD confirmed "₹23-24 Cr from NPCIL currently" — nuclear already contributes ~8% of FY25 revenue. Not priced in by market; no analyst has noted this.
- **FY25 data correction: revenue ₹287.82 Cr** (down 22% from FY24 ₹370.76 Cr), but profit grew 12.4% to ₹16.51 Cr — margin expansion on lower revenue = structural cost improvement, not top-line weakness.
- **Cyclical not structural:** ₹200-250 Cr in active bids in pipeline as of AGM date. Management cited tariff uncertainty + global near-recession + India election year. Same explanation peers (ISGEC, Thermax) gave. Order book = ₹223.70 Cr Sep 2025.
- **Exotic materials bay = higher-margin niche:** Dedicated bay for inalloy/super duplex — "competition is less, value addition is more." Capital-light initiative (existing shed converted), margin accretive.
- **Borrowing headroom raised to ₹500 Cr** — current debt only ~₹90 Cr. Signals management is preparing infrastructure for scale-up without equity dilution. Gauy plant expansion next in queue.

**Portfolio signal:** PATELSAIR — if holding, AGM confirms hold. At 10.5x P/E on FY25 audited earnings (market shows 16.3x on TTM which is cyclically depressed), the stock is cheaper than the screener headline suggests. Key trigger: Q4 FY26 results, May 2026.

### 2026-04-15 — "The Worst Is Over" | Ravi Dharamshi, Value Quest

[Full Summary](soniashenoyofficial/NA_iKmTGMthTDY_summary.md) · [Raw Transcript](soniashenoyofficial/NA_iKmTGMthTDY.md) · [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iKmTGMthTDY)

- **FCNR bonds = $34B in 3 months in 2013** — government can run this playbook again; Dharamshi estimates $100B potential this time. This is the policy response trigger to watch.

**Portfolio signal:** HDFCBANK — Dharamshi most positive on large private banks as credit cycle revives. Validates holding. RSYSTEMS/SAKSOFT/NEWGEN — revisit growth assumptions; IT sector headwind is structural, not a dip.
