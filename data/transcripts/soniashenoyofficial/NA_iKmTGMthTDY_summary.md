## Guest & Context
Ravi Dharamshi, founder of Value Quest Investment Advisors. Long-term equity fund manager, manages ~₹350+ Cr. Known for macro-informed bottom-up investing.

## Macro View
- US actions (tariffs, Iran war) all flow from one root cause: unsustainable US debt → forced to inflate + suppress rates (financial repression). Tariffs = revenue mechanism to fund interest payments.
- World moving from unipolar (US hegemony) to transactional/negotiation-based order. Whoever has higher pain tolerance survives.
- Iran war = US trying to control Strait of Hormuz → leverage over China (gets 40% of oil from Iran/Venezuela). Not primarily about Iran — it's about China containment.
- Two things that can stop Trump: (1) popular opinion / midterm elections (Nov), (2) bond yields above 4.5-4.6% — bond market is the only force he backs off for.
- India's vicious cycle: crude up → rupee depreciates → FII outflows → RBI can't inject liquidity → banking system tight → growth slows.

## Sector Bets (3-5 year horizon)
- **Bullish — Renewables/Energy independence:** Most strategic theme. India needs 50-70% renewable energy mix (currently 5-10% of generation). Solar is backbone. 10-15 year journey. Sector needs protectionism to backward integrate — acceptable cost.
- **Bullish — Defence/Aerospace/Space:** Strategic necessity, not just a trade. India must build competency here.
- **Bullish — Semiconductors / EMS:** Part of supply chain resilience build.
- **Bullish — Large private sector banks / NBFCs:** Deleveraged balance sheets, credit growth revival underway (8-9% → 13-15% before this crisis interrupted).
- **Bullish — Data centers:** Private sector capex happening here.
- **Cautious — IT services:** AI disruption structural, not cyclical. 40-50% productivity improvement → job losses.

## Key Numbers & Data Points
- FII outflows Sep 2024 to now: ~$45B from $5.5T market cap = 0.9% of MCap, ~5% of foreign holdings → comparable to GFC 2008 episode. Historically this marks the bottom.
- Every $10 crude rise = $18-20B extra cost for India annually.
- If annual crude goes from $65-70 → $85-90: $25-30B extra burden → fiscal deficit widens from 4.3% toward 4.6-4.7%.
- War scenario: 4-8 weeks = priced in. Beyond 8 weeks = unpriced, structural damage begins.
- IT sector: 7M employees (IT services + BPO/GCC combined). AI productivity gain 40-80%. Could displace 1-1.5M people over 3-5 years.
- FCNR bonds in 2013: raised $34B in 3 months. A repeat could bring $100B+ this time.
- FII ownership of Indian equities: was 25% of market, now down to ~15%. 10 percentage points of structural outflow.
- 8th Pay Commission: comes into effect in ~12 months → consumption catalyst.

## Contrarian / Interesting Takes
1. **Bond market > Trump:** The mechanism that actually limits Trump's actions is bond yields going above 4.5%, not geopolitics or popular opinion. This is a specific, measurable signal to watch.
2. **FII outflows = GFC equivalent, but India is 4x bigger:** The absolute damage ($45B) looks like 2008, but India's economy and market are 4x the size — the relative damage is much smaller. This is why he's bullish.
3. **Renewables are NOT a free market trade:** He explicitly says don't invest in renewables on pure economics. They need protectionism to survive AND they're a strategic national necessity. That combination = sustained support regardless of economics.

## One-Line Verdict
Buy India equities with a 3-5 year horizon — $45B FII outflow has already matched the GFC episode, war damage is priced for a 4-8 week scenario, and the next upcycle starts when government announces the policy response (FCNR bonds, LTCG cut, FDI opening).
