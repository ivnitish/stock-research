**19 consecutive years of dividend.** Raised from 10% in 2006 to 30% now — management is financially disciplined.

## Nuclear — CONFIRMED CURRENT REVENUE (Critical Finding)

Management confirmed in Q&A:

> *"We are already a vendor for nuclear power corporation of India and even in existing we have a good about 23 or 24 cr from NPCL... if the new projects when they come on surface when the procurement starts yes we don't see any hurdles as being a vendor we regularly receive their tenders and their request for quotations... we are positioned very well to take advantage of that situation."*

**Key implication:** NPCIL is not just potential — Patels is *currently* generating ₹23-24 Cr from NPCIL, which is ~8% of FY25 revenue. Nuclear is already in the P&L, not just on the horizon. The larger India nuclear expansion (100 GW target) would represent a step-change from this base.

## Expansion Initiatives

### New Gauy Plant (Alakari, Massan, Gujarat)
- 4 sheds completed, 11,006 sq mt total covered area
- Total capex: ~₹24 Cr (multi-year)
- FY25 capex: ₹2.24 Cr (₹1.25 Cr plant & equipment)
- **Planning further expansion:** "We are thinking to expand our facilities in Gauy plant... another bay, another shade... going forward with the expansion" — will be funded from internal accruals + existing credit limits, no equity dilution signalled

### Exotic Materials Bay (Rakenpur)
- Converted an existing bay specifically for inalloy and super duplex stainless steel
- Rationale: these materials require dedicated handling (no contamination with carbon steel)
- Margin advantage: *"We can process in tonnage less but we can get much more value addition... competition is a little bit less compared to regular steel products"*
- Near-term capex minimal; bay already built

### Foreign Collaborations
- Blanket MOU-style arrangements (not formal JVs) with foreign engineering consultants, including Tyson group
- Project-based, not tech transfer or long-term tie-up
- These help access GCC/Southeast Asia projects through back-to-back arrangements

## First Principles Stress Test

**Management's cyclical explanation holds up:**
- India oil & gas capex did slow through H2 FY25 — corroborated by ISGEC, Thermax commentary in same period
- Global near-recession fears (US tariffs, Mar-Apr 2025) suppressed customer capex orders — not specific to Patels
- FY26 deceleration = industry-wide, not company-specific order loss

**What would have to be wrong:**
- If competitors are gaining share specifically on Patels' sweet spot (₹5-50 Cr projects) — no evidence of this
- If the ₹200-250 Cr bid pipeline doesn't convert — need to see Q4 FY26 and Q1 FY27

**Key risk not addressed by management:** The ₹116 Cr contingent liabilities. No question was raised on this. This remains the largest unmonitored tail risk.
