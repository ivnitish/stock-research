## 2. Core Philosophy

- **Earnings growth is the master of all stock prices.** 100% probability that stock price increases if net profit grows over long periods — everything else follows from this.
- **Good business ≠ good stock.** Many great businesses (HUL, Kotak) are bad stocks at expensive valuations. You need good business + reasonable price.
- Investment horizon: minimum 2-3 years. No trading or technical analysis.
- Markets move in pendulums — extremes of pessimism = best entry points.
- Equity returns are lumpy, not linear.

## 3. Six Growth Triggers to Look For

### A. Capacity Expansion
- Check Fixed Assets + CWIP growth on balance sheet quarter by quarter
- Profitable capex = demand exists before capacity added
- Entry point: 1-2 quarters BEFORE capex goes live (before market sees it)
- Example: Vima Labs — entered one quarter before capex live, made 102% in one year

### B. Operating Leverage
- Fixed costs stay same; incremental sales flow directly to profit
- Signal: Margins grow FASTER than sales growth
- High utilization (90%+) = constrained → new capex = growth trigger
- Low utilization (50-60%) = room to grow margins without capex

### C. Margin Expansion
- Sustained higher margins over multiple quarters = pricing power
- Watch R&D spend as % of sales (5%+ = serious capability building)
- Buy 1-2 quarters before new products go live, not 2-3 years before
- Example: SRF moved from 12% EBITDA (commodity pharma) → 25%+ EBITDA (CDMO)

### D. Promoter Activity
- Promoters sell for many reasons; promoters BUY only when stock is cheap
- Signal: Repeated buying over days/weeks accumulating to ₹1-2 Cr = meaningful
- Track via screener.in promoter activity section
- Example: Polycable — promoter bought before capacity expansion went live

### E. Regulatory/Government Tailwinds
- Government focus areas = policy support + faster approvals + international partnerships
- Import bans / anti-dumping duties can transform economics overnight
- Read 4-5 newspapers daily (3 domestic, 2 international) to track policy shifts
- Examples: Dixon Technologies (PLI), Amber Enterprises (PLI electronics)

### F. Turnaround
- Debt restructuring → lower interest costs → profit improvement
- New CEO with strong track record
- Shedding loss-making divisions
- Entry at extreme pessimism = best alpha
- Example: CG Power — Vai and Subaya took from bankruptcy to growth

## 4. Moat Framework (Six Types)

| Moat Type | How to Identify | Example |
|-----------|----------------|---------|
| **Brand/Pricing Power** | Market share growing; premium pricing; cult creation | Royal Enfield (share 15%→32% FY15→FY26; ROE 30%) |
| **Network Effects** | Fixed cost, asset-light; margins expand as users grow | BSE — OPM expanded from mid-40s → 60%+ as daily turnover rose |
| **Process/Execution** | Complex, multi-step value chain; hard to replicate | Navin Fluorine — florination process enabled pharma drug entry |
| **IP/Patents** | R&D spend >5% of sales; new patents being filed | Loris Labs — 10yr CDMO capability building, explosive growth when theme hit |
| **Cost Advantage** | Backward integration; scale barriers | Privy Specialty Chemicals — CST method, only 4-5 global players, 15-20% cost edge |
| **Working Capital** | Negative working capital; collect before you pay | FMCG companies historically |

**Key rule:** ROCE >18-20% = structurally sound. (Nominal GDP ~11-12%; you want 50% premium = 18%+ threshold.)

## 5. Valuation Framework

### P/S (Price to Sales) — For Loss-Making or High-Growth Companies

| EBITDA Margin | Fair P/S Multiple |
|---------------|-----------------|
| 1-10% | 0.5x–1.0x |
| 10-15% | 1.0x–2.0x |
| 15-20% | 2.0x–4.0x |
| 25-30% | 8.0x–10.0x |
| 30%+ | 8.0x–10.0x+ |

### Other Metrics
- **EV/EBITDA** — For capital-intensive businesses (accounts for debt)
- **P/E** — Normalize for one-off items; use forward P/E
- **PEG ratio** — P/E ÷ expected growth. PEG <1.0x = potentially undervalued; >2.0x = full valuation
- **P/B** — For banks/NBFCs; low P/B NOT always good (check quality of book)
- **Reverse DCF** — What growth rate is the market pricing in? If market implies 20% but you forecast 15%, thesis already priced

### When to Sell
- Valuation exceeds 5-7 years of growth priced in
- Management guidance misses actuals repeatedly
- Valuation exceeds long-term average (example: Vima Labs at 7x sales → exit)
- Thesis invalidated — exit ruthlessly

## 6. Corporate Governance — Non-Negotiables

**Red Flags to screen for immediately:**
- OCF / Net Income conversion <70-80% (3-year average) = earnings quality concern
  - High receivables + high sales = potential fake billing
  - High inventory + modest sales = overvalued inventory
- Promoter pledge of significant portion of equity
- Related party transactions at non-market rates (artificially inflates profits)
- Auditor qualifications in annual report
- Material contingent liabilities (hidden tax/legal)
- Promoter doing business in personal name (benefiting personally, not company)

**Quote:** "A crook will never let you make money. Be most particular about corporate governance. Be ruthless."

## 9. Things to Implement in Our Research Process

1. **Capacity Utilization + CWIP Tracker** — Add fixed assets + CWIP growth table to every research note. Flag inflection when new capex goes live.

2. **OCF/Net Income Conversion Check** — Calculate 3-year average. Flag if <70-80%. Add to quality score.

3. **"Walk the Talk" Tracker** — For every holding, create guidance vs. actual results log. Grade management execution. Can use NotebookLM with past concall transcripts.

4. **Operating Leverage Test** — Always check: do margins grow faster than sales? If not, growth is not creating leverage = weaker business.

5. **Reverse Moat Questions** — For every holding, document: How long will moat last? What early warning sign of erosion? Quarterly competitive scan.

6. **Government Policy Score** — Rate each stock 1-5 on policy backing. Track policy tailwinds/headwinds monthly.

7. **Thesis Invalidation Triggers** — For every investment, define explicit red lines. If crossed, exit. No hoping.

8. **PEG Ratio Addition** — Start computing PEG for all positions (P/E ÷ forward growth rate). Flag if >2.0x.

9. **P/S Benchmarking Table** — Use the margin → P/S table above to sanity-check valuation on any stock irrespective of profitability stage.

10. **Promoter Activity Check** — For all new ideas, explicitly track promoter buying/selling on screener.in before entering. Meaningful buy = repeated accumulation over weeks.
