1. Business Summary

Diversified conglomerate with primary focus on power (75% of revenue) and ferro alloys. Key assets: 434 MW thermal power in India (5 plants across Telangana, Odisha, AP) + 300 MW Maamba Collieries in Zambia (PPAs secured). Also operates ferro alloys, mining, agribusiness, and healthcare segments. The thesis is a value + growth play: low P/E (18x) for a business generating strong profits, with Zambia 300MW expansion adding significant new capacity.

3. Why This Could Be a Multi-Bagger

  • Catalyst 1: Zambia 300MW expansion. Doubling Maamba capacity from 300→600 MW in a power-starved country. Long-term PPAs + captive coal = very high-margin power. If executed, this is a significant earnings boost.
  • Catalyst 2: India power upcycle. PLI factories, EV charging, data centers all need power. NAVA's existing capacity benefits from spot market premiums during peak demand.
  • Catalyst 3: Conglomerate unlocking. Non-power segments (ferro alloys, mining) could be value-unlocked via demerger or separate listing.
  • Catalyst 4: Current undervaluation. DCF bear case (minimal growth) gives ₹671 — 18% above current price. The stock is genuinely cheap on fundamentals.
