# Funding Options — Personal OD / Flexi vs MTF vs Personal Loan

Working notes on borrowing/leverage options for my situation. Rates as of **July 2026** — always re-verify on the lender's app / pre-approved offer before acting.

## My constraints
- CIBIL ~800, no FD (so no OD-against-FD)
- Primary bank: HDFC, **linked to Groww** (settlements, MTF/loans)
- **Hard rule:** no flexi/OD on the HDFC account linked to Groww — keep market money and OD money on separate banks
- Existing leverage sits on Groww (MTF and/or loan)
- Want a Flexi/OD-style line: interest only on amount drawn, re-withdrawable

---

## 1. Quick decision tree

- **Need leverage for stocks** → use **MTF**, not a personal loan/flexi. Cheaper and no end-use breach. Move to **Dhan (~12.49%)** vs Groww (14.95%).
- **Need a lump sum for a known expense** (renovation, wedding, debt payoff) → **plain personal loan**, cheapest rate wins → **IDFC FIRST ~10%**.
- **Need a standby cash buffer** (draw occasionally, stay well under limit) → **Flexi/Dropline OD** — worth a higher rate *because you only pay on what you draw*.
- **IPO funding** → only meaningful for sHNI/HNI; route OD→savings→ASBA; use a dedicated IPO-funding loan for HNI, not personal flexi.

## 3. Personal Loan vs Flexi — the utilization break-even

A plain PL charges interest on the **whole** amount from day 1. A Flexi charges only on what you **draw**. So a higher-rate flexi can still be cheaper if you keep it lightly drawn.

**Break-even:** Flexi is cheaper when average drawdown < `(PL rate ÷ Flexi rate)` of the limit.

Example — IDFC PL @10% vs Tata Flexi @14.5%: break-even = 10 ÷ 14.5 = **~69%**.

| Avg utilization of limit | Cheaper option |
|---|---|
| Full amount, kept drawn (lump-sum need) | **Plain PL @10%** |
| ~70%+ drawn on average | Plain PL |
| <~69% drawn (standby buffer) | **Flexi @14.5%** |
| Rarely / small draws | Flexi wins big |

Worked (₹10L limit): full draw → PL ₹1.0L/yr vs Flexi ₹1.45L/yr (PL wins). Standby avg ₹4L drawn → Flexi 14.5%×4L = ₹58k/yr vs PL 10%×10L = ₹1.0L/yr (Flexi wins).

⚠️ **Tata drawdown fee caveat:** if Tata charges ~4.5% on each dropped-down amount, the flexi advantage collapses — get the exact fee schedule in writing first.

---

## 4. Product type to ask for

- **Flexi Dropline / "Flexi Term Loan"** = closest to a true OD: interest only on drawn, **re-withdrawable** within the limit during the flexi tenure. **← ask for this.**
- **Flexi Hybrid** = same, but interest-only EMIs initially, then principal+interest. Lower early outflow.
- **Plain PL** = full disbursal, no re-withdraw. Only for a defined lump-sum need.

## 5. Account setup (keeps money trails separate)

- Open **one clean savings account** for OD money + IPO/ASBA, at a bank **other than** the HDFC-Groww account.
- Cleanest: take **IDFC FIRST** and open an **IDFC FIRST savings** as the disbursal + ASBA account — good rate, one relationship, fully separate from HDFC-Groww.
- Groww stays wired only to HDFC. Flexi lands in the second account.

---

## 6. Stock leverage — stick to MTF, not flexi

- Personal flexi at 13–16% is equal-to-worse than MTF **and** breaches capital-market end-use T&Cs.
- Cheapest MTF: **Dhan 12.49%** vs Groww 14.95% (~2.5% saving).
- Only reason to prefer unsecured flexi over MTF: **MTF lets the broker force-sell** your shares on a margin call; unsecured flexi (no pledge) **cannot touch your demat**. Cheaper leverage = MTF (accept force-sell risk); no-pledge leverage = flexi (accept higher rate).
- **Moving existing shares to Dhan:** you can't "transfer" a funded MTF position — it's pledged. Options: (a) clear Groww funding → unpledge → transfer delivery shares to Dhan → re-pledge (small CDSL fees each side), or (b) sell & rebuy (triggers tax — usually not worth it). **Better: leave existing Groww MTF to run down; route NEW MTF through Dhan.** *(Rate/tier confirmation for Dhan still pending — verify current slab on Dhan app.)*

---

## 7. IPO angle

- **Path works:** flexi drawdown → your savings a/c → ASBA (funds *blocked*, not debited). Can't ASBA directly from an NBFC flexi — must route through a bank account.
- **Cost if not allotted:** block releases in full, but you still pay flexi interest for the ~5–7 days the money sat drawn.
- **Retail cap ₹2L/PAN** — funding it is barely worth the interest. Only meaningful in **sHNI/HNI** category.
- **For HNI IPO:** use a dedicated **IPO-funding loan** (7-day NBFC product, low effective cost), not personal flexi.
- **T&Cs:** bank PL/OD almost always bans capital-market/IPO use; Bajaj NBFC money routed via own savings breaks the trail, enforcement weak in practice. **Call: fine for occasional retail/sHNI ASBA; not a routine leverage engine.**

---

## 8. Approval / FOIR watch-outs

- **MTF exposure is usually NOT reported to CIBIL** (broker margin funding, not a loan account) → won't hit FOIR. But a Groww **loan/LAS IS reported** and counts.
- Lenders cap **FOIR ≈ 50–55%** of net monthly income across all EMIs.
- **Check pre-approved offers first (soft pull).** Space applications — multiple hard inquiries in weeks dents an 800 score.

---

## 9. Do / Don't

- ✅ Ask for **Flexi Dropline**, disbursed to a **non-HDFC-Groww** account
- ✅ Compare on **ROI + annual/renewal + drawdown fee together**, not headline rate
- ✅ Check **pre-approved** offers first (soft pull)
- ✅ For stock leverage use **Dhan MTF**, not flexi
- ✅ Keep one **dedicated savings account** for OD money + ASBA
- ✅ Take the **shortest tenure** you can afford — long tenure multiplies total interest
- ❌ Don't take **Tata OD** if the ~4.5%/drawdown fee applies
- ❌ Don't pay **>14.5% bank / >16% NBFC** flexi
- ❌ Don't route bank-OD money into IPO/shares — T&C breach, real audit trail
- ❌ Don't fire multiple loan applications the same week
- ❌ Don't use flexi as a standing stock-leverage line — wrong tool, wrong cost
