2026-04-01 — The Iran War, Oil Shock, and What It Means for Us

The Big Picture

We are living through the largest oil supply disruption since the 1973 Arab oil embargo. The US and Israel launched a surprise attack on Iran on February 28, 2026. Iran retaliated by closing the Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of the world's oil flows. Brent crude went from $71 to $116 in one month. India imports 88% of its crude. This is the single most important macro variable for Indian equities right now.

1. The Iran War — Full Timeline

The conflict is now in its 5th week with no clear end in sight.

Phase 1: The Strike (Feb 28 - Mar 3)

  • Feb 28, 2026: US and Israel launched surprise airstrikes on sites and cities across Iran, codenamed "Epic Fury." Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei assassinated along with several senior Iranian officials.
  • Mar 1-2: Iran retaliated with missile and drone strikes against Israel, US bases (Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar), and Saudi Aramco's Ras Tanura refinery.
  • Brent: Jumped 8% from $71.32 to $77.24.

Phase 2: Strait of Hormuz Closure (Mar 4 - Mar 12)

  • Mar 4: Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz "closed" — attacked civilian ships attempting transit. ~17.8 million barrels/day of oil flow disrupted.
  • Mar 8: Brent crossed $100/bbl for the first time since 2022.
  • Mar 10-12: Gulf state oil production (Kuwait, Iraq, Saudi Arabia, UAE) collectively dropped by 10+ million barrels/day. This is the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market.
  • Mar 12: Iran's new supreme leader declared the Strait must remain closed. Brent hit $100.
  • Brent: $100+ and rising.

Phase 3: Escalation and Ultimatum (Mar 13 - Mar 31)

  • Mar 13-14: US flew B-52 bombers over Iranian territory for the first time, targeting ammunition depots and air bases in Isfahan. Day 15 of continuous US-Israel strikes.
  • Mar 19: ECB postponed planned rate cuts, raised 2026 inflation forecast, cut GDP growth projections.
  • Mar 20: Iraq declared force majeure on oil exports. Brent topped $112.
  • Mar 21: War entered its 4th week with no end in sight (NPR).
  • Mar 23: Trump announced a 5-day pause on strikes against Iranian power plants to steady oil prices. Extended deadline to April 6.
  • Mar 27-28: Brent settled at $112.57 (+36% from pre-war levels). US gasoline crossed $4/gallon for first time since 2022.
  • Mar 30: Iran accused the US of preparing a ground invasion. Brent topped $116.
  • Mar 31 (Day 32): Iran threatened US tech companies. Kuwaiti oil tanker attacked. CNN reports war continues.

What's Next: The April 6 Deadline

Trump has said: if Iran does not reopen the Strait of Hormuz by April 6, the US will conduct "extensive attacks on Iranian energy sites." This is the key date.

  • If Hormuz reopens: Oil could drop to $85-95. Markets rally.
  • If strikes on Iranian energy sites (Kharg Island): Oil could hit $120-200/bbl (Bloomberg, Morgan Stanley). Global recession risk.
  • Analysts warn: stopgap measures (strategic reserves, production rerouting) lose effectiveness in early-to-mid April.

2. Oil Price Impact on India

Current: Brent ~$112-116/bbl (up from $71 pre-war, +60%)

Why India is uniquely vulnerable:

  • India imports 88% of crude oil — 3rd largest importer globally
  • Every $10/bbl increase adds ~₹40,000 Cr to the import bill
  • Current account deficit widens → rupee depreciates → imported inflation
  • ICRA warns oil above $100 could derail India's 4.5% FY27 fiscal deficit target
  • India was forced to reduce Russian oil imports (Trump linked lower US tariffs to this condition) — removing the discount cushion India relied on since 2022
  • Premium petrol/diesel hiked ₹1.50-11/litre on April 1, 2026

4. India-Pakistan: Fragile De-escalation (Background Risk)

The acute India-Pakistan crisis is separate from the Iran war but compounds geopolitical uncertainty.

Timeline: - Apr 22, 2025: Pahalgam terror attack — 26 Hindu tourists killed in J&K - May 2025: 4-day India-Pakistan air war (missiles/drones on military bases). Most serious confrontation in ~30 years. US-brokered ceasefire. - Jan 2026: Diplomatic handshake in Dhaka — faint dialogue restart - Apr 2026 (now): Cold standoff. No active fighting. US intel still flags nuclear risk. CFR warns another terror incident could re-trigger.

Market impact: The conflict premium has mostly dissipated. Defence stocks (GRSE, KERNEX) benefit from elevated spending. Risk: sudden re-escalation → 5-10% Nifty selloff.
