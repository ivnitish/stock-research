KILL: 4 names off the list

Danlaw Technologies

Screener shows current PE at 96.6 (the 21x figure in the CSV was standalone-only). 5-year sales CAGR only 25%, negative 3-year ROE (-3.8%), no dividend despite reported profits, WC days deteriorating. Stock trades at 15x book with earnings quality issues (other income inflating). This is not a compounder — the CSV mis-classified it.

Sunlite Recycling

Non-ferrous metal recycling with operating margin of 2%. Even after H1 FY26 sales doubled to ₹1,669 Cr, net profit is ₹27 Cr. The business is a commodity spread trade — buy scrap, sell refined metal, arbitrage the delta. Any dip in aluminium/copper prices wipes the wafer-thin margin. The +156% 1-year stock return is a re-rating on volume growth that isn't defensible. Kill.

One Global Service Provider

Promoter holding path is a red flag: 29.5% (2021-24) → 15% (Mar 2025) → 68.4% (Jun 2026). That's not organic — it's a reverse takeover or open offer or major QIP. Screener data hidden behind login further complicates DD. ROCE 87% is suspiciously extreme for a healthcare services name. Cannot underwrite a 10-bagger thesis on a company whose ownership just churned. Kill.

Pajson Agro

Incorporated 2021, only 3 quarters of listed data visible. CFO −14 Cr against Net Profit ₹25 Cr in FY26. Working capital days expanded 47 → 123. Too new + too much cash-flow stress to distinguish real growth from working-capital inflated revenue. Wait for 6-8 quarters of listed history. Kill for now.

WATCH: 3 names, wait for cash flow to catch up

JD Cables (Electricals)

Real growth: FY22-FY26 sales grew 55 → 365 Cr (79% CAGR), profits 0.4 → 32 Cr (170% CAGR). ROE 52%. Promoter holding steady at 70%. Cables was the #2 winning sector last 6m.

The problem: CFO is −13 → −18 → −74 Cr over FY24-25-26 against Net Profit of 5 / 22 / 32. Debt going 5 → 57 Cr. Working capital days 49 → 113. This is classic aggressive-growth-eats-cash pattern. In cables, this can work (long orders, big receivables) but the cash burn has to reverse within 2-3 quarters or the story becomes a debt story. Watch for FY27 Q1 — if CFO turns positive, revisit.

Taurian MPS (Industrial Products)

Similar shape: sales 4 → 99 Cr in 4 years (109% CAGR), profits went from -2 to +16 Cr. But CFO −37 Cr in FY26, WC days 150 → 229, debt 9 → 25 Cr. Same growth-eating-cash pattern. Watch.

Avana Electrosystems (Heavy Electrical)

Cleanest metrics of the whole list on paper — sales 5y CAGR 41%, profit CAGR 113%, OPM expanded 4% → 21%, debt reduced 9 → 1 Cr, CFO/Op ratio improving to 72-85%. Promoter 74%.

The problem: IPO'd on NSE SME 20 Jan 2026 — just 6 months of listed history. IPO subscribed 132× (extreme retail froth). Pre-listing financials are unaudited-by-market. Wait 2-3 quarters of listed reporting before committing capital. Real quality candidate but too raw.

DEEP-DIVE: 3 names worth full research

1. Sahana Systems (IT Enabled Services)

Numbers:

  • Sales FY23-26: 24 → 69 → 167 → 331 Cr (139% CAGR)
  • Profits: 6 → 18 → 39 → 75 Cr (122% CAGR)
  • Current OPM 29% (industry-leading for IT services)
  • ROE 33%, 3y avg
  • PE 12 vs industry 27 — cheap
  • MCap 813 Cr

Catalyst live: Board approved 1:5 bonus + mainboard migration + capital hike on 22 June 2026. Mainboard migration is a real re-rating trigger — SME names typically gain 30-50% of terminal multiple on migration alone as institutional buyers become eligible.

Yellow flags:

  • Promoter holding drifted 62.9 → 57.3% over 3 years — dilution, not selling, but worth tracking
  • CFO volatile: 41 Cr FY26 vs 75 Cr profit (55%). Working capital days rose 101 → 149
  • WC increase is normal for a scaling IT services firm but track for stability

What to verify next: concall transcript on FY27 growth guidance, client concentration (top-5 client share), pipeline visibility. IT services 10x requires either offshore-to-nearshore mix change or platform product tailwind. Check both.

2. Ganesh Green Bharat (Solar Manufacturing + EPC)

Numbers:

  • FY26 revenue ₹1,068 Cr vs FY25 ₹318 Cr — 232% YoY growth
  • FY26 PAT growth 149%
  • FY27 guidance ₹1,500-1,700 Cr (40-59% growth)
  • Order book ₹2,210 Cr (jumped from ₹700 Cr in 24 hours in late May 2026) — 2× current revenue visibility
  • ROE 32%, ROCE 37%
  • PE 8.99 — dramatically cheap
  • Promoter 73% stable, debt manageable at 52 Cr

The concern: EBITDA margin moderated 15.9% → 10.6% as project mix shifted to lower-margin SITC (supply, install, testing, commissioning) and manufacturing scaled. Stock is down 49% from 52-week high — market punished the margin compression.

The re-rating case: Order book coverage is exceptional. Solar EPC is a real Modi-government-priority theme (500 GW renewable by 2030). If FY27 margins stabilize at 10-11% on ₹1,600 Cr revenue → PAT ~₹165 Cr vs ₹75 Cr FY26 = 120% profit growth. At even 20x PE that's ₹3,300 Cr mcap vs current ₹676 Cr = ~5x in 12-18 months before we discuss multi-year 10x.

What to verify next: FY27 Q1 margin trajectory (Aug 2026 release), order book execution rate, manufacturing capacity utilization. Also review pv-magazine and industry sources on solar module pricing — thin margin means module ASP moves matter.

3. Neetu Yoshi (Castings & Forgings — Railway)

Numbers:

  • Sales FY23-26: 16 → 47 → 70 → 98 Cr (82% CAGR)
  • Profits: 0 → 13 → 16 → 25 Cr (from a low base)
  • ROE 40%, ROCE 32%
  • "Almost debt free" — borrowings 17 → 14 → 4 Cr
  • Promoter 70% stable
  • PE 25 (higher than the other two but justified by growth)
  • MCap 624 Cr

The story is the pipeline:

  • ₹14.76 Cr wagon manufacturing order (May 2026)
  • RDSO approval for high-speed train bogies + couplers (March 2026) — this is the key. RDSO approval is a multi-year moat and unlocks Vande Bharat / Bullet Train supply
  • Rail Coach Factory Kapurthala CCA (June 2026) valid till 2030 for FIAT Bogie Brake Support
  • ₹27.48 Cr raised via convertible warrants + separate ₹51 Cr raise for new Kanpur plant (target 250 Cr/year turnover)
  • Domestic order Rs 7.39 Cr cast steel bearing plates

The concern: Cash conversion is deteriorating — CFO/Profit went 58% → 56% → 31%. Working capital days 70 → 134. This is the classic "big orders + big receivables" small-cap trap.

The upside: If Kanpur plant delivers 250 Cr on top of current 98 Cr, revenue trebles. At current 25% net margin that's ₹85 Cr PAT vs ₹25 Cr today. Railway multi-baggers historically get 30-50x PE (Titagarh, Jupiter, Texmaco during upcycles). Ceiling could be 5-7x in 2-3 years if capacity executes.

What to verify next: Kanpur capex timeline (commissioning date), first order from Integral Coach Factory / Rail Coach Factory post-RDSO approval, working capital normalization by FY27 Q2.
