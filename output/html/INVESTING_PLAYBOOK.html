🎯 The 5 Focus Areas (in priority order)

1. Concentration — cap at 12 positions

Damani says 8-12, Iyer says max 15. You're at 23 right now. Your brain can't track 23 properly — that's not discipline failure, that's working-memory math.

Rule: every new buy pushes the weakest existing slot out. The 12-slot list (below) is finite. Forces ranking.

2. Exit discipline — 7-day rule

A written exit decision becomes an order within 7 days, OR is formally reversed in writing with a stated reason. No third option (which is "I'll do it later" — that's how SWIGGY sat for 30 days).

When the urge to delay shows up: the cost of executing today vs. perfectly timing the exit is small. The cost of carrying dead weight for 30 more days is much larger (mental bandwidth + opportunity cost + framework drift).

3. Entry discipline — only buy at asymmetry ≥ 2x

Per your own CLAUDE.md Phase 5.3:

  • BUY (5-8%) requires asymmetry ≥3x + MOS ≥20%
  • BUY reduced (3-5%) requires asymmetry 2-3x + MOS 10-20%
  • TRACKING POSITION (1-2%) is for asymmetry 1.5-2x with strong thesis

Stop opening positions in the 1.0-1.5x range — that's where mediocrity lives. If the math is sub-1.5x, the right answer is wait or skip, not "buy a small bit."

4. Reading depth — 1 deep concall per week, no exceptions

30% CAGR comes from knowing one thing the market doesn't. That edge is earned, not bought. Pick ONE holding's concall PDF every Sunday, read cover-to-cover, write 3 bullets in the research log. Same time, same day, same format — ADHD-friendly.

The three concalls listed in High Priority above are this and next week's reads.

5. Cycle out winners that hit fair value

GRSE +29% above base case ₹2,459. SHILCTECH similarly above. Holding past fair value isn't conviction — it's inertia.

Rule: when CMP crosses 0.95× of base case, write the trim decision in the research file with a 30-day execution deadline. Capital recycled into the next high-asymmetry name.

🛡️ Rules I Commit To (re-read when tempted)

1. Maximum 12 positions. Tracking stubs don't count. If I want to add a 13th, I must close the weakest first.

2. Maximum 12-15% in any single position. No single stock can wipe me out.

3. No leverage. No F&O. No margin. Period.

4. No buying on news days. Wait until Sunday.

5. No averaging down losers until I've revisited the original thesis and found it still intact.

6. Tax-loss harvest annually — every March, sell every loser to offset gains.

7. Written decision before order. If I can't articulate the reason in DECISION_LOG.md, the trade isn't ready.

8. No "interesting, worth watching" conclusions. Per framework Instruction 8 — that's paralysis. Decide BUY/TRACKING/AVOID with size.

9. Cap research notes at 11 sections. Beyond that, I'm decorating, not analyzing.

10. Quarterly review is sacred. First Saturday of Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct. Cancel anything else.

🚫 The "Don't" List (when ADHD brain is loud)

  • ❌ Don't read "experts" predicting the market
  • ❌ Don't compare your portfolio to NIFTY weekly — quarterly is enough
  • ❌ Don't research a stock you read about on Twitter — wait 7 days; if still relevant, then deep-research it
  • ❌ Don't average down a Grade C — averaging down is for Grade A/B with intact thesis only
  • ❌ Don't take a "small position" in something you didn't research properly — that's a permission slip to research it later, which you won't
  • ❌ Don't buy 30 minutes before market close
  • ❌ Don't re-research a stock you exited — closure is part of discipline
