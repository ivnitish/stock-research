Thesis read. This is the cleanest "thesis confirmed" print of the three holdings being reviewed. The five-quarter quarterly trend (Q4 FY25 ₹541 Cr → Q4 FY26 ₹647 Cr) shows zero seasonality reversal — every quarter has been larger than the last. OPM expansion of 200 bps over the year is operating leverage in the literal sense: fixed overhead at the head-office and land-development level is being absorbed over a 22% larger revenue base. This is not a residential developer pattern (which is lumpy, project-completion driven) — it is starting to look like the IT-park + data-centre annuity stream is now a meaningful part of the mix even though Anant Raj doesn't disclose segment data cleanly.

Material catalyst — Haryana ₹25,000 Cr MoU. On 01-Jun-2026 the company announced an MoU with the Haryana state government for ₹25,000 Cr of investment in data centre and cloud services infrastructure. For context, this MoU value alone is ~10x current mcap. MoUs are not contracts and Indian state-government MoUs historically convert at very low rates — the realistic conversion expectation should be 20-40% over 5-7 years, not headline value. But two things are now true that were not true in March:

1. The data centre pivot is no longer optionality — it is an explicit, government-anchored capital plan with a number attached. The thesis line "data center optionality is not yet priced in" needs revision; some of it is now being priced in (hence the +17% stock move).

2. State backing in Haryana matters because land conversion, power allocation, and water permissions — the three actual bottlenecks for data centre buildout — are state-government decisions. An MoU with Haryana is the regulatory derisking step ahead of commercial commitments from hyperscalers.

Position-size implication. Current position is 200 sh = ~7.7% of portfolio at ₹549. Q4 was strong, FY26 was strong, the data centre catalyst converted from optionality to in-motion.
