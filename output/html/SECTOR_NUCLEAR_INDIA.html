1. The Theme: Why Nuclear, Why Now?

India has announced one of the most ambitious nuclear expansion programmes in history:

MetricCurrentTargetTimelineMultiple
Installed nuclear capacity8.8 GW100 GWBy 204711x
Intermediate target8.8 GW22.4 GWBy 2031-322.5x
Under construction now—13.6 GWVarious stages—
Nuclear share of total power~2%~15-20% (est.)By 20478-10x

Why this is different from past announcements:

  • SHANTI Bill 2025 — for the first time in India's history, private companies can own and operate nuclear plants (49% equity cap). This breaks the NPCIL monopoly.
  • Budget allocation: ₹20,000 Cr for Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) alone
  • Liability reform: Removal of supplier "right of recourse" — this was THE blocker for international vendors (Westinghouse, GE). Now resolved.
  • Fleet mode execution: 10 identical 700 MW PHWRs being built simultaneously to reduce cost through learning curves
  • Repurposing old thermal plants: Government will use retired coal plant land for nuclear — removes land acquisition bottleneck

The maths of the opportunity:

  • Adding ~91 GW of new capacity (from 8.8 GW to 100 GW)
  • At ₹15,000-25,000 Cr per GW (indigenous to foreign-assisted)
  • Total capital expenditure: ₹14-23 lakh Cr ($170-280 billion) over 21 years
  • Even the intermediate target (22.4 GW by 2032) implies ~₹2 lakh Cr of spending in the next 6 years

This is comparable to India's entire defence capital budget for 5 years. The companies positioned in this supply chain will see order books swell over the next decade.

Tier 3: Shipyard Connection (GRSE link)

Nuclear plants need heavy equipment transported, and the broader energy infrastructure buildout supports shipyard demand. Additionally, nuclear-powered submarines are a separate but related opportunity for defence shipyards (GRSE, Mazagon Dock).

4. The Madhu Kela Framework Applied

Madhu Kela's approach: find themes where the government is committed to spend (irreversible policy), then find companies with:

1. Monopoly or near-monopoly position in a critical supply chain link

2. Certification moats (nuclear certifications take 3-5 years to get)

3. Small enough that the order book moves the needle (L&T won't 2x because of nuclear; Walchandnagar might)
