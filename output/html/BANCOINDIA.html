1. Business Summary

India's leading manufacturer of automotive cooling and sealing products. Core products: radiators, oil coolers, intercoolers, AC condensers, charge air coolers, and gaskets. Serves major OEMs (Tata Motors, M&M, Maruti, Ashok Leyland, commercial vehicle players) and exports to global markets. Asset-light manufacturing with high ROE. The thesis is India's auto sector growth + export opportunity in thermal management components, which are becoming more critical in both ICE and hybrid/EV powertrains.

3. Why This Could Be a Multi-Bagger

  • Catalyst 1: India CV cycle recovery. Commercial vehicles (trucks, buses) use more cooling systems per vehicle. India's logistics infrastructure build-out + infrastructure spending = CV volume recovery = higher demand for Banco's products.
  • Catalyst 2: Export expansion. Banco already exports to global OEMs. European OEMs are diversifying supply chains away from China (post-2022). Indian quality auto component suppliers with IATF certifications are prime beneficiaries.
  • Catalyst 3: EV/Hybrid thermal management. Battery thermal management in EVs is a growing TAM. Banco's core competency (heat exchange) directly applies. Not an EV threat — it's an opportunity.
  • Catalyst 4: Operating leverage. With 32% ROE and minimal capex needs, incremental revenue flows through to profit efficiently. Not a capital-intensive business.

The Valuation Verdict

The two models give different reads:

  • DCF says fairly valued: Implied 13.2% growth is the base case — company has delivered 39% CAGR, so 13% is conservative. DCF doesn't look expensive.
  • P/B-ROE says fair in bull: Base (30% sustained ROE) gives ₹407, but ROE is currently 32% — so the "base" scenario is already being achieved today.

Key insight: P/B-ROE base underestimates because it uses 30% when actual ROE is 32%. At 32% sustained ROE, justified P/B = (0.32-0.05)/(0.12-0.05) = 3.86x → Fair value = ₹114 × 3.86 = ₹440. The current price of ₹576 prices in ROE slightly above current levels — not unreasonable.

Verdict: Roughly fairly valued. Not cheap, but not expensive. This is a high-quality business at a fair price — Munger's ideal.
