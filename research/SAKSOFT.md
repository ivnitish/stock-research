#### What the Q4 print actually says

Margin held up beautifully — 19% FY26 OPM is the highest in the visible history of the company and the 300 bps YoY Q4 expansion is real. PAT growth at 22% on revenue growth of 14% is the operating-leverage pattern that justified the entry thesis. So far, so good.

But the top-line tells a different story than the headline. Revenue went ₹249 → ₹258 → ₹251 → ₹249 across Sep'25 / Sep'25-revised / Dec'25 / Mar'26. That's not acceleration. That's a plateau over three quarters. YoY growth in Q4 is +3.8% — the slowest quarterly YoY in this file's history. FY26 revenue growth (14%) is below the 5-year revenue CAGR of 21%. The compounder thesis written in March 2026 ("every quarter better than the last") is no longer being delivered.

The 22% PAT growth came almost entirely from margin expansion, not from revenue scaling. Margin expansion is a finite source of compounding — once OPM is 19%, getting to 22-23% is harder than going from 17% to 19%.

#### Multi-bagger math: ₹129 → ₹320 (2.5x) in 3 years

The portfolio model's +31.7% expected 3-year CAGR implies EPS roughly doubles in 3 years (₹10 → ₹20) at constant multiple, or EPS up 50-60% with modest re-rating. Forward-looking math:

- **If FY26's 22% PAT growth sustains for 3 years:** EPS ₹10.05 → ₹18.2 by FY29. At P/E 14.6x (current) = ₹266. At 18x (mild re-rating) = ₹327. The model target is achievable but requires sustained 22% PAT growth.
- **If revenue keeps decelerating to 10% and OPM plateaus at 19%:** EPS grows ~10% — ₹10 → ₹13.4 by FY29. At 14.6x = ₹196. That's a 1.5x in 3 years, ~14% CAGR — well below the model's 31.7%.

The data point that worries me: revenue has been flat for three quarters. The model's 31.7% CAGR is implicitly assuming the operating leverage continues AND revenue re-accelerates. Q4 says revenue is not re-accelerating right now.

#### Promotion verdict — HOLD CURRENT (5%)

The reasons NOT to promote to 10% on this print:

1. **Top-line deceleration is a yellow flag, not a green light.** ₹249 Cr four quarters running is a plateau. Three more quarters of revenue plateau and the 22% PAT trajectory breaks because there's no more OPM to mine.
2. **No deal-pipeline / TCV disclosure to underwrite re-acceleration.** Saksoft doesn't disclose order book or TCV. Without that, the 31.7% model CAGR is a curve-fit on a 5-year CAGR that's no longer being delivered.
3. **PEG 0.66x is attractive but not unique enough to size up.** P/E 14.6x ÷ 22% PAT growth = 0.66 is decent but not screaming. EPACK and other Top-5 candidates have stronger forward visibility.
4. **Existing 5% is the right size for a Grade B compounder with execution proof but no moat.** Promotion to 10% requires either Grade A quality (which IT services doesn't get to per the score) OR a stronger margin-of-safety / forward-visibility case.

What would change my mind in the next 1-2 quarters: (a) revenue back above ₹275 Cr/quarter (i.e., a clean break above ₹258 Sep'25 peak), OR (b) a disclosed large deal win / TCV print that gives forward visibility, OR (c) D/E reverting back below 0.10x as the FY25 debt build-up unwinds. Without one of these, this stays a 5% position and I revisit at Q1 FY27 results.
