## 1. Business Summary

Mumbai-based company operating from a 70-acre freehold campus in Goregaon East — one of Mumbai's premium commercial hubs near Western Express Highway and the airport. Four core segments plus one emerging:

1. **Nesco IT Park** (~50% revenue, ~85% PBIT margin) — 1.87M sq ft leasable area. Blue-chip tenants: HSBC, KPMG, PwC, MSCI, BlackRock, Framestore. Annuity rental income with built-in escalations.
2. **Bombay Exhibition Centre (BEC)** (~25% revenue, ~51% margin) — India's largest private-sector exhibition venue. 60,000 sqm. Advance rent collection model. Historically 80%+ margins, now normalizing as Jio World Centre competes.
3. **Foods Division** (~20% revenue, ~12% margin) — Catering during exhibitions (20,000+ meals/day during events). Growing fast (106% YoY in recent quarters). Low margin but incremental profit.
4. **Indabrator** (~3% revenue, loss-making) — Shot blasting equipment. Legacy, immaterial.
5. **Highway Amenities** (new segment, early stage) — Nesco entering highway wayside amenities (rest stops, food courts, fuel, convenience retail on national highways). Announced 2026.

**The thesis is simple: irreplaceable land + annuity income + Tower 2 expansion.**

### Highway Amenities Assessment

**Business rationale from Nesco's perspective:** The company has deep expertise in operating large-format hospitality + F&B + commercial spaces (BEC, Foods catering, and now the Tower 2 hotel). Highway amenities extend this competency to a new asset class. Government of India's national highway development (NHAI target: bharatmala) creates demand for quality wayside stops.

**Why this is NOT the same as core Nesco:**
- Freehold land moat does NOT apply — highway amenity sites are leased from NHAI, not owned
- Margins are thin (fuel: near-zero; F&B: 15-20%; convenience: 25-30%) vs IT Park (85%) or BEC (51%)
- Capital-intensive per site (₹15-30 Cr per site for construction + equipment)
- Competition: IOCL, HP, BPCL already operate network; private players like Tata, Jubilant, Emerald also entering
- Regulatory dependence: NHAI concession terms, fuel pricing controls

**Assessment:** If Nesco operates 10-15 high-quality amenity stops, revenue could be ₹80-150 Cr at scale, but PAT contribution minimal (5-8% net margin → ₹4-12 Cr). **Capital watch threshold:** Tower 2 costs ₹3,500 Cr over FY26-30. If management discloses >₹200 Cr committed to highway amenities before Tower 2 is complete, that is a capital discipline red flag — downgrade Management score from 4 → 3.

**Current impact on thesis:** Neutral to slightly negative. Does NOT change the core IT Park + Tower 2 thesis. Monitor Q4 FY26 concall.

## 3. Compounding Engine — Q&A

### Q1. Is incremental ROIC genuinely high (>20%) — and is it structural, not cyclical?

**ROIC calculation (FY25):**
- NOPAT = EBIT × (1 - tax rate) = ₹438 Cr operating profit × (1 - 25%) = **₹329 Cr**
- Invested Capital = Total Equity + Borrowings - Cash/Investments = ₹2,799 + ₹110 - ₹1,321 = **₹1,588 Cr**
- **ROIC = 329 / 1,588 = 20.7%**

**Incremental ROIC (3-year delta, FY22 → FY25):**
```
NOPAT FY25 = ₹329 Cr | NOPAT FY22 = ₹220 Cr × 0.75 = ₹165 Cr
Change in NOPAT = ₹164 Cr

Invested Capital FY25 = ₹1,588 Cr | Invested Capital FY22 = ~₹1,050 Cr (est.)
Change in IC = ₹538 Cr

Incremental ROIC = 164 / 538 = 30.5%
```

**Incremental ROIC of 30.5% is excellent.** Every rupee of new capital deployed over 3 years generated 30 paise of additional annual after-tax profit.

**Why it's structural (not cyclical):**
- **Irreplaceable land:** 70 freehold acres acquired decades ago at negligible cost. The land cost in the denominator is essentially zero — any revenue from this land generates near-infinite return on original investment.
- **85% IT park PBIT margin:** Once a floor of an IT park is leased, the marginal cost of maintaining that lease is minimal (maintenance, property tax, security). Rents escalate 5-7% every 3 years contractually.
- **Annuity model:** Blue-chip tenants (HSBC, KPMG, BlackRock) sign 9-year leases with 3-year escalation clauses. Revenue is contracted, not discovered.
- **Zero debt amplifies returns:** No interest cost means all operating profit flows to equity holders.

**Honest check:** If IT park occupancy drops below 75% for 2+ quarters, ROIC declines below 15% because fixed costs (property tax, maintenance, staff) don't scale down. This would indicate structural demand weakness in Mumbai office market.

### Q3. What's the implied compounding math?

**Compounding formula:**
```
Reinvestment Rate: 88%
ROIC on incremental capital: 30.5%
Implied earnings CAGR = 88% × 30.5% = 26.8%
```

**But this overstates near-term growth** because Tower 2 capex is a cash drain NOW with returns arriving in FY30+. The actual earnings trajectory is:
- FY26-FY29: Revenue grows 10-15% (IT Park escalations + BEC + Foods), but OPM compresses from 60% to ~50% (Foods mix). PAT CAGR ~10-12%.
- FY30-FY34: Tower 2 commissions. Revenue jumps 40-60% as 5M sq ft fills. PAT could double in 2-3 years.

**Realistic blended 7-year CAGR: 15-18%** (front-loaded capex, back-loaded returns).

At 17% compounding for 7 years → 3.0x on intrinsic value.
Entry at 18x P/E → exit at 25x P/E (re-rating on proven Tower 2 execution) → **total return ~4.0x** over 7 years.

**Smell test:** Yes, this passes. The 15-18% CAGR is conservative vs the 26.8% theoretical because it accounts for the capex lag. Tower 2 is a step-function catalyst, not smooth compounding.

## 6. Competitive Landscape

### Market Position

Nesco occupies a unique niche: it's neither a pure developer (doesn't sell), nor a REIT (no distribution mandate), nor a pure office player. It's a **freehold land + annuity rental + exhibition** business. The 70-acre campus in Goregaon is a one-of-one asset in Mumbai — no comparable integrated IT park + exhibition + hotel campus exists at this scale in the city.

### Structural Advantages vs Peers

1. **Irreplaceable land:** 70 freehold acres in Goregaon → No developer can create this. Prestige/Brigade can buy new land, but at ₹200-300 Cr/acre current Goregaon rates, the capital required would be ₹14,000-21,000 Cr just for the land. → Quantified effect: land cost in ROIC denominator is near-zero, which is why Nesco achieves 85% IT Park margins vs 25-30% for developers who buy land at market rates.

2. **Zero debt tradition:** → While Embassy REIT carries ₹10,000+ Cr debt and most developers are leveraged 0.5-1.0x D/E, Nesco has been essentially debt-free for decades. → Quantified: zero interest cost means 100% of EBIT flows to pre-tax profit. Peers lose 15-25% of EBIT to interest.

3. **No selling risk / annuity model:** → Prestige/Brigade/Sunteck depend on property sales cycles (lumpy, cyclical). Nesco earns pure annuity rental — recession-resistant once occupied. → Quantified: revenue variance in Nesco (excl. BEC seasonality) is <10% quarter-to-quarter vs 30-50% variance for developers.

4. **No distribution mandate:** → Unlike REITs that must distribute 90%+ of income, Nesco retains 88% of earnings and redeploys into Tower 2 without raising equity. → Quantified: retained ₹329 Cr in FY25 for reinvestment vs Embassy distributing ₹2,500+ Cr.

### Why Multiples Differ + Re-rating Thesis

**NESCO at 18x vs Prestige at 50x:**

- **Policy tailwind:** Mumbai GCC demand (Global Capability Centres) + data centre demand creates structural office demand for Goregaon. Government infrastructure (Metro connectivity to Goregaon) improves tenant access.
