## 1. Business Summary

Swan Defence and Heavy Industries Ltd (SWANDEF) is India's largest private shipbuilder by infrastructure — the 662 × 65 m dry dock at Pipavav, Gujarat, capable of handling vessels up to 4,00,000 DWT. This is among the world's 10 largest dry docks by volume.

The company has a troubled 28-year history under four different owner groups (Pipavav Shipyard → Pipavav Defence → Anil Ambani's Reliance Naval → IBC resolution). The current promoter — Swan Energy's Nikhil Merchant (via SPV Hazel Infra Ltd) — acquired the asset through NCLT resolution in December 2022 for ~₹2,100 Cr and took operational control in January 2024.

As of April 2026, the company has restarted ship repairs (August 2024) and won its first major commercial contracts: 6 chemical tankers for Stenersen (Norway, $227M), 4 ammonia bulk carriers for Energy ONE (₹1,500–3,000 Cr), a naval training ship for Oman, 5 OSV completions for San Maritime, and a teaming agreement with Mazagon Dock for LPD construction. Revenue remains near-zero in P&L as shipbuilding follows percentage-of-completion recognition — actual P&L revenues are 2-5 years away.

## 5. Valuation

**Current price: ₹1,789 | Market Cap: ₹9,909 Cr**

Standard DCF/P/E models are inapplicable — the company has no earnings and won't for 3-4 years. Three relevant frameworks:

### Framework 1: Asset-Based (Replacement Cost)

| Scenario | Asset Replacement Value | Less: Debt | Equity Value | Per Share | vs CMP |
|----------|------------------------|------------|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Bear (distress, 30% haircut) | ₹3,500 Cr | ₹2,615 Cr | ₹885 Cr | ₹168 | -91% |
| Base (fair replacement ~₹6,000 Cr) | ₹6,000 Cr | ₹2,615 Cr | ₹3,385 Cr | ₹643 | -64% |
| Bull (strategic buyer at full value) | ₹8,000 Cr | ₹1,500 Cr (refinanced) | ₹6,500 Cr | ₹1,233 | -31% |

### Framework 2: Order Book Optionality (EV/Backlog)

Global shipyards trade at 0.3–0.7x backlog EV. With ₹4,500 Cr confirmed backlog + ₹2,615 Cr debt:
- At 0.3x backlog: EV = ₹1,350 Cr; equity value = -₹1,265 Cr → zero
- At 0.7x backlog: EV = ₹3,150 Cr; equity value = ₹535 Cr → ₹101/share
- At 1.0x backlog (premium): EV = ₹4,500 Cr; equity value = ₹1,885 Cr → ₹358/share

### Framework 3: FY30 Earnings Discounted Back

- Base FY30 PAT: ₹75 Cr (commercial only, ₹150 Cr interest after partial refi)
- At 25x P/E: ₹1,875 Cr mcap → PV at 15% discount = ₹930 Cr = **₹177/share**
- Bull FY30 PAT: ₹375 Cr (with Navy contract, ₹100 Cr interest)
- At 35x P/E: ₹13,125 Cr → PV = ₹6,520 Cr = **₹1,237/share**

**Synthesis:** Fundamental fair value range ₹200–1,250/share. CMP ₹1,789. The stock is justifiable ONLY in the Navy contract bull case, and even then barely. Current price assumes the bull case is almost certain — it is not.
