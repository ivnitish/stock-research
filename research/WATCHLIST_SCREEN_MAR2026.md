## Deep Dive Candidates: Top 3 With Margin of Safety

### 1. Just Dial — The Highest Conviction Value Pick

**Why interesting:** P/E 7.8x, P/B 0.92x (below book value!), 46% net margins, ₹4,473 Cr market cap. Trading at 50% off 52-week high.

**What is Just Dial?** Local search + business discovery platform. 150M+ users. Was a high-flyer, fell on concerns about competition from Google/Jio. Now recovering with subscription model + SME services.

**Why it could be cheap:**
- Cash-rich balance sheet (book value ₹574/share, stock at ₹526 = trading below book)
- 46% net margins are exceptional (basically all revenue drops to profit at scale)
- Revenue growing slowly (6.4%) but stable — not declining

**Key risks:** Structural competition from Google Local, Jio Business. Revenue growth has been flat for years. The "platform" MOAT is questionable.

**Quick valuation:**
- EPS implied: ₹526 / 7.8x P/E = ₹67/share
- At 15x P/E: ₹1,005/share (91% upside)
- At 12x P/E + stable business: ₹804/share (53% upside)
- Even at 10x P/E (conservative): ₹670/share (27% upside = 20-30% MOS territory)

**Verdict: Worth full thesis. Buy range: ₹450-550. The below-book-value case is compelling for a profitable, cash-generating business.**

### 2. Balmer Lawrie — Cheap PSU With Steady Cash Flows

**Why interesting:** P/E 10.8x, P/B 1.48x, 10.4% net margin, government-owned (80% stake). Revenue growing slowly (4.2%), earnings stable.

**What is Balmer Lawrie?** Diversified PSU: industrial packaging (drums), logistics infrastructure (cold chain, warehouses), lubricants, travel services. ~₹2,889 Cr market cap.

**Why it could be cheap:**
- P/E 10.8x for a consistently profitable business = clear value
- PSU with 80% government holding — not going anywhere
- Dividends: PSUs often yield 3-5% at these valuations
- Trading 29% below 52-week high

**Key risks:** PSU capital allocation (government may redirect profits). Revenue growth is anaemic (4.2%). Not a growth story — pure value/dividend.

**Quick valuation:**
- Net profit ~₹300 Cr × 15x P/E = ₹4,500 Cr fair value vs ₹2,889 Cr current = **56% upside**
- 20-30% margin of safety clearly present at current price

**Verdict: Value + dividend play. Not a multi-bagger but solid 20-30% upside + dividends. Worth small position.**

### 3. Triveni Engineering — Corporate Action + Earnings Doubling

**Why interesting:** P/E 25.9x, P/B 2.58x, earnings growth 97.9%, revenue growth 16.5%, forward P/E 19.4x. Corporate action: Power Transmission business demerger (275/share implied value).

**What is Triveni Engineering?** Sugar + Power Transmission (gears, turbines, water treatment). The Power Transmission segment is high-quality, capital-light, and deserves a premium multiple. Sugar is cyclical.

**The corporate action angle:**
- Demerger of Power Transmission at ₹275/share book value
- If Sugar is valued at 10x P/E and Power Transmission at 30x P/E (engineering specialty)
- The combined value could be significantly higher than current market cap

**Key risks:** Sugar is cyclical and government-controlled (FRP prices, ethanol policy). Demerger timeline unclear.

**Verdict: Corporate action creates value unlock potential. Earnings doubling (97.9%) with forward P/E 19.4x is reasonable. Monitor demerger progress.**
