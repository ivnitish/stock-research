## 1. Business Summary

Manufacturer of power and distribution transformers for India's electricity transmission and distribution (T&D) infrastructure. Based in Vadodara, Gujarat. Products: distribution transformers (11kV, 33kV) and power transformers (up to 220kV). Key customers: State Electricity Boards (SEBs), central utilities (PGCIL), industrial clients. Also expanding into ferrite core transformers (used in electronics, EV chargers, telecom). The thesis is India's power infrastructure buildout — government's ₹3 lakh crore+ power sector investment plan, rural electrification (RDSS scheme), and renewable energy integration requiring T&D upgrades.

## 3. Why This Could Be a Multi-Bagger

- **Catalyst 1: India's T&D infrastructure buildout.** RDSS scheme alone is ₹3.03 lakh Cr investment in distribution upgrades. Every new substation needs transformers. Structural 5-7 year demand tailwind.
- **Catalyst 2: Renewable energy integration.** Solar and wind power requires extensive T&D infrastructure — transformers at every grid connection point. India targeting 500 GW RE by 2030.
- **Catalyst 3: Data center boom.** Hyperscalers (Google, Microsoft, Amazon) building India data centers. Each data center requires high-quality power transformers. Premium product = premium margin.
- **Catalyst 4: Ferrite transformer expansion.** Electronics-grade ferrite transformers used in EV chargers, telecom, industrial electronics. Higher value-add, higher margin than utility transformers. If this segment grows, could materially expand the TAM.
- **Catalyst 5: Small float, high quality.** Only ~1.14 Cr shares outstanding. As mutual funds and institutions discover this company, re-rating possible.

## 5. Valuation

- **Current Price:** ₹3,856
- **Market Cap:** ~₹4,400 Cr

### Model 1: DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)

*Base FCF: ₹185 Cr | Shares: 1.14 Cr | Discount rate: 12% | Terminal growth: 5%*

| Scenario | Growth Rates (5yr) | Fair Value | vs ₹3,856 |
|----------|-------------------|------------|-----------|
| Bear | 5%→3% declining | ₹2,064 | -46% |
| Base | 12%→6% declining | ₹2,613 | -32% |
| Bull | 20%→10% declining | ₹3,596 | -7% |

**Reverse DCF:** Market implies **~20-22% FCF CAGR** for 5 years. Revenue growth is only 10.7% — but if revenue accelerates with T&D buildout + margin holds at 30%, this is achievable.

### Model 2: P/B-ROE (Justified Price-to-Book)

*Book Value: ₹367 | Cost of Equity: 12% | Terminal growth: 5%*

| Scenario | Sustainable ROE | Justified P/B | Fair Value | vs ₹3,856 |
|----------|----------------|---------------|------------|-----------|
| Bear | 30% (ROE falls from 44%) | 3.57x | ₹1,310 | -66% |
| Base | 44% (ROE holds) | 5.57x | ₹2,044 | -47% |
| Bull | 55% (ROE improves) | 7.14x | ₹2,621 | -32% |

**Implied ROE at P/B 10.5x:** ~78% — nearly double the current 44%. That seems impossible.

### The Valuation Problem

Both models agree: **Shilchar is expensive at current levels.** However, there are caveats:

1. **Historical ROE of 44% with zero debt** — the P/B-ROE model may not capture the full value of a zero-debt manufacturer with superior margins
2. **Small cap + discovered recently** — the stock may have re-rated sharply as the power theme played out. Price reflects the theme premium, not just the business.
3. **DCF bull case (-7%)** suggests if FCF grows at 20%+ CAGR (plausible with T&D buildout), current price is roughly fair

**Verdict:** Expensive on simple models, but the quality is real. This is a "buy the quality, not the price" situation — best to HOLD existing position and look to add only on a correction to ₹2,500-₹3,000.
