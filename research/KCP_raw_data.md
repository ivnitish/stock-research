## 12. BUSINESS SEGMENTS OVERVIEW

### A. CEMENT (Primary Revenue Driver — Standalone)
- **Plants:** Macherla & Muktyala, Andhra Pradesh
- **Capacity:** 4.3 MTPA combined
- **Clinker capacity:** Being upgraded to 10,000+ MT/day with WHR
- **Market share:** ~0.61% of India cement industry (small player)
- **Geography:** Southern India, primarily AP/Telangana
- **Standalone revenue proxy:** FY25 Rs 1,387 Cr (includes engineering + hotel + captive power)
- **OPM% (standalone):** Highly volatile: 22% (FY21) -> 14% (FY22) -> 1% (FY23) -> 6% (FY24) -> 2% (FY25)
- **CRISIL notes:** "Sub-par operating performance of cement segment" and "demand-supply mismatch in South India cement market"

**Capex underway:**
1. **WHR Plant:** 16 MW (15.8 MW) at Muktyala. Total project ~Rs 235 Cr (user estimate), Rs 145 Cr spent. 5-year payback expected.
2. **Railway Siding:** At Muktyala to reduce logistics costs
3. **Total capex envelope:** Rs 750-850 Cr over FY26-FY28 (CRISIL)
4. **Expansion:** New greenfield third plant proposed in AP (Rs ~400 Cr for 3.5 MTPA expansion per news)

### B. HEAVY ENGINEERING (Chennai, Thiruvottiyur)
- **Location:** Thiruvottiyur, Chennai — one of the largest integrated HE workshops in India
- **Established:** 1955 (seven decades)
- **Serves:** Cement, sugar, mineral, power, steel, oil & gas, defence, space, nuclear
- **ISRO association:** 3+ decades; supplies flight hardware — motor cases, nozzle divergent structures, inter-stage structures
- **Gaganyaan:** Fabricated Integrated Air Drop Test Crew Module (IADT-CM) for ISRO. First module: 3,120 kg, 3.1m dia, 2.6m height, aluminum + 15CDV6 steel. Two modules ordered.
- **Financial history:** Cumulative Rs 112 Cr loss over 10 years (user noted), now turning profitable
- **Q3 FY26 standalone:** Cement segment EBIT Rs 13.59 Cr vs loss of Rs 28.15 Cr previously (this may include engineering in the segment reporting)
- **Land:** ~75 acres in Thiruvottiyur, Chennai. Only 25-30 acres occupied by engineering operations. Remaining 45-50 acres potentially monetizable.

### C. HOTEL (Mercure Hyderabad KCP)
- **Brand:** Mercure (Accor Hotels managed)
- **Location:** Banjara Hills, Hyderabad overlooking Hussain Sagar Lake
- **Rooms:** 127-128 rooms (Superior 98, Deluxe 11, Suites 8)
- **Amenities:** 3 restaurants, 5 meeting spaces, gym, terrace rooftop bar
- **Revenue contribution:** Small (likely Rs 30-50 Cr range, not separately disclosed in available data)

### D. SUGAR — KCP VIETNAM INDUSTRIES LIMITED (Subsidiary)
- **Established:** 1997 in Vietnam
- **Location:** Son Hoa district, Phu Yen province
- **Current capacity:** 10,000 TCD (Son Hoa factory) + 1,000 TCD (Dong Xuan factory)
- **Biomass power:** 30 MW operational
- **Total investment to date:** USD 103 million
- **Farming footprint:** ~20,000+ hectares, ~10,000 households
- **Expansion plan:**
  - Crushing: 10,000 -> 15,000 TCD at Son Hoa
  - Biomass power: 30 MW -> 75 MW (45 MW Phase 2)
  - Additional investment: USD 60 million
- **Profitability:** This is the crown jewel. Difference between consolidated PAT (Rs 253 Cr) and standalone PAT (Rs -2 Cr) in FY25 implies Vietnam contributed ~Rs 255 Cr in profit.
- **The standalone "Investments" line shows only Rs 25 Cr** — this is the historical cost of the Vietnam investment. The subsidiary's actual equity/net worth is multiples higher.

## 19. RED FLAGS / RISKS TO INVESTIGATE

1. **Standalone business is barely profitable** — FY25 standalone PAT was Rs -2 Cr. The India operations (cement + engineering + hotel) are struggling.
2. **Cement OPM collapsed:** From 22% (FY21) to 2% (FY25 standalone). South India cement market has supply glut.
3. **Heavy engineering has had cumulative Rs 112 Cr losses** over 10 years — drag on consolidated returns.
4. **Over-reliance on Vietnam subsidiary** for profitability — single-country, single-commodity (sugar) risk.
5. **Borrowings jumped** from Rs 542 Cr to Rs 825 Cr (consolidated) in 6 months — capex-driven but watch debt trajectory.
6. **Low dividend payout** (0.2% yield) despite net cash — promoter not returning capital to shareholders.
7. **Promoter holding at 44.25%** — not low but not strong either. No recent buyback activity noted.
8. **Conglomerate structure** = inherent complexity discount. Market may never fully value sum-of-parts.
9. **Vietnam currency risk** — profits earned in Vietnamese Dong, reported in INR.
10. **Working capital intensive:** 175-day cash conversion cycle on consolidated basis.
