## 1. Business Summary

DCM Shriram International Ltd is a freshly carved-out entity created by the three-way demerger of DCM Shriram Industries Ltd (DCMSRIND, listed since 1974), effective record date December 26, 2025, listed February 17, 2026. Shareholders of DCMSRIND received 1 share each of DCMSIL and DCM Shriram Fine Chemicals Ltd (DSFCL) for every share held.

**DCMSIL contains four businesses:**

1. **Industrial Fibre — Shriram Rayons, Kota (Rajasthan):** Rayon tyre cord yarn, greige/treated/dipped fabric, chafer fabric, nylon chafer — reinforcement materials for high-performance radial and bias tyres. Established 1965 with Beaunit Fibers (USA) technology. Top supplier to 2 of the world's top 6 tyre companies, supplying to 45+ plants globally. Earned 16 Export Awards. Key moat: technically specialised product, sticky customer relationships, limited Indian competition.

2. **Defence — ZEBU Armoured Vehicles:** Multi-Role Armoured Vehicles (MRAVs) and Light Bullet-Proof Vehicles (LBPVs) under the ZEBU brand, targeting Indian Army, CAPF, State Police, and homeland security. Industrial licences granted under Arms Act 1959 (DIPP). Manufacturing facility under development at Kota.

3. **Defence — Drones (Zyrone Dynamics):** 30% stake acquired in Zyrone Dynamics (Turkey-based UAV manufacturer) at $3.5M total company valuation. Develops multi-role UAVs for defence, surveillance, agriculture, and industrial applications. Expanding product lines within 24 months.

4. **Shipping Containers + Engineering Services:** ISO-certified containers, precision contract manufacturing, industrial research.

**What DCMSIL is NOT:** It does not contain DCM Shriram Industries' sugar, alcohol, power, or chemical segments — those remain in DCMSRIND and DSFCL respectively.

## 3. Why This Could Be a Multi-Bagger

The thesis is simple but requires patience: a newly listed, newly demerged industrial conglomerate with a real defence optionality, bought at trough earnings.

**Catalyst 1 — Defence Revenue Materialisation (ZEBU + Drones):** India's army and CAPF need to replace thousands of ageing vehicles. ZEBU's MRAV targets this market with Make in India credential. If ZEBU wins even 500 units over 5 years at ₹1–2 Cr/unit = ₹500–1,000 Cr revenue, the entire DCMSIL market cap gets replicated in one segment alone. The Zyrone Dynamics drone partnership positions DCMSIL to supply UAVs for defence and surveillance — a market that is explicitly PLI-eligible and import-banned. Even a ₹100 Cr drone order would be meaningful at current market cap.

**Catalyst 2 — P/E Re-Rating on Defence Classification:** DCMSIL currently trades at 9x P/E on FY25 because the market sees it as a rayon + containers industrial company. If defence revenues become 20%+ of the top line with higher margins, the multiple could re-rate to 20–25x. At ₹63 Cr PAT (FY25) × 25x = ₹1,575 Cr mcap — a near-3x from current ₹558 Cr without any earnings growth.

**Catalyst 3 — FY26 Recovery + Normalised Earnings.** The compression to ₹4 Cr PAT in 9M FY26 (from ₹63 Cr in FY25) is extreme and unexplained. If this is project-timing related in rayon or a one-time issue in containers, FY27 PAT could return to ₹50–70 Cr. At 20x P/E on recovery = ₹1,000–1,400 Cr mcap = 2x from current levels from earnings alone.

**Combined scenario (defence + recovery + re-rating):** FY28 PAT ₹80–100 Cr at 25x P/E = ₹2,000–2,500 Cr mcap = **3.5–4.5x from current ₹558 Cr.** This is the realistic multi-bagger case — not blue sky.

## 6. Competitive Position

### Rayon Tyre Cord (Core Business)
- One of very few domestic producers in India; premium technical product
- Top supplier to 2 of world's top 6 tyre companies (likely Bridgestone/Michelin or Goodyear/Continental)
- 45+ global plant relationships → switching cost is real (tyre companies qualify suppliers rigorously; re-qualification takes 12–24 months)
- **Risk:** Rayon losing ground to synthetic in lower-performance tyres; but high-performance radials still need rayon chafer — this is a stable-to-modest-growth end market

### Defence Armoured Vehicles (ZEBU)
- Competing with: Tata Advanced Systems, Mahindra Defence, AVNL/OFB (government)
- DCMSIL advantage: Make in India positioning, captive manufacturing facility, DCM Shriram group relationships in government
- Disadvantage: No delivery track record vs Tata (has delivered vehicles to Army)
- Key opportunity: Ministry of Home Affairs (CAPF) vehicle replacement — DCMSIL specifically mentions this segment

### Drones (Zyrone Dynamics, 30% stake)
- Zyrone is a small Turkish maker ($3.5M valuation for 100% = negligible entry cost)
- India's PLI for drones + import ban on certain drone categories = right timing
- Early stage; options on a growing market at very low cost basis
- Competitors: ideaForge, Throttle, Garuda Aerospace — all better funded

## 7. The FY26 Compression — Key Unanswered Question

**This is the central risk to the thesis.** FY25 PAT was ₹63 Cr. 9M FY26 PAT is ₹4 Cr. That is a 94% collapse in profitability. Possible explanations:
1. **Project timing** — rayon tyre cord orders lumpy; large customer order shipped in FY25 creating a high base
2. **Margin squeeze** — raw material (cotton/wood pulp) cost spike reducing rayon margins
3. **Container business slowdown** — global container demand cyclical
4. **One-time items** — plant maintenance shutdown, forex, new segment ramp-up costs

Without a concall (company listed only Feb 2026, no calls yet), this is unverifiable. **The Q4 FY26 results (May/June 2026) are the critical data point.** If Q4 PAT ≥ ₹15 Cr, FY26 full year ~₹20 Cr → recovery likely in FY27. If Q4 < ₹10 Cr → structural problem, revise thesis to AVOID.
