## Summary Verdict

### Why this business?

Unichem is a 30-year-old Mumbai pharma company that built a respectable India branded prescription portfolio over decades, then sold it entirely to Torrent Pharma in November 2017 for ‚āĻ3,600 Cr (cash deal, ~12x EV/EBITDA of the branded business). What remains is the generic-export company ‚ÄĒ APIs and finished-dosage formulations sold mostly into the US through their own ANDA filings, plus Europe and emerging markets, manufactured at five plants (Roha, Pithampur, Goa, Baddi, Ghaziabad). Post-sale the Daga family deployed the cash into capacity expansion and buybacks. Eight years on, the company's revenue (‚āĻ2,202 Cr FY26) is back near where it was pre-sale (‚āĻ2,400 Cr FY18 with the branded business included) but the quality of earnings is structurally worse ‚ÄĒ ROCE has dropped from the mid-teens (with branded India) to single digits (without).

The physical bet would be that the FY25 PAT recovery (‚āĻ138 Cr, OPM 13%) reflected a step-change in the US generic book ‚ÄĒ new ANDA approvals, complex-generics getting paid, capacity utilisation finally rising at Pithampur. The Q3 and Q4 FY26 reversal to losses says otherwise. Without a clear physical mechanism explaining what changed in FY25 vs what reverted in FY26, the recovery has to be assumed cyclical until proven structural.

### Strengths

- **Promoter holding up from 50.94% (Jun 2023) to 70.22% (Mar 2026)** ‚ÄĒ Daga family bought back 19pp via open-market accumulation over 30 months. This is the Kamayaka promoter-buying signal at scale ‚ÄĒ promoters don't put ‚āĻ500 Cr+ of own capital into a stock they think is going to ‚āĻ200 from ‚āĻ400. It is the strongest single argument for the file.
- **Trades at 0.89x P/B** ‚ÄĒ book value ‚āĻ385/share against ‚āĻ343 CMP. The ‚āĻ2,703 Cr reserves include the post-Torrent cash and the historic capacity build at Pithampur (USFDA approved) and Roha (API). On liquidation arithmetic the downside is bounded.
- **No major USFDA enforcement action** ‚ÄĒ Roha API plant inspection June 2025 closed with three Form 483 observations described by the company as procedural (not data-integrity); no warning letter on file at any plant. - **Diversified geographies** ‚ÄĒ US, Europe, EM, Latam exposure means no single customer >30% (per past filings); FDA-only risk doesn't take the whole company.

### Concerns

- **The FY25 recovery just reversed.** Q1 FY26 was clean (PAT ‚āĻ42 Cr), Q2 was passable (‚āĻ38 Cr), then Q3 ‚ąí‚āĻ12 Cr and Q4 ‚ąí‚āĻ10 Cr. ## Detailed Analysis

### Deep Dive A ‚ÄĒ The Torrent Cash Question

The ‚āĻ3,600 Cr received in November 2017 has compounded at sub-3% over eight years if you measure by current operating earnings power. The Daga family's defence would be that the cash funded capacity expansion at Pithampur and Roha that's now producing the US generic book ‚ÄĒ and that book delivered ‚āĻ253 Cr PAT in FY26 (10% return on the cash, before the H2 reversal). The empirical test of whether the capacity build was value-accretive is the FY27 print. If H1 FY26 (PAT ‚āĻ80 Cr) was the real run-rate and H2 was an inventory write-down or one-time cost, capacity is paying back at ~16% pre-tax ‚ÄĒ acceptable. If H2 FY26 is the run-rate, capacity has paid back at sub-5% and the buyback was the right capital allocation call but the operating capex was not.

### Deep Dive B ‚ÄĒ Promoter Buyback as Signal

Daga family went from 50.94% (Jun 2023) to 70.22% (Mar 2026) ‚ÄĒ accumulating ~19pp over 30 months, mostly via open-market purchases per BSE shareholding pattern disclosures (open-offer ~10% in 2024 + ongoing creeping). At average prices ‚āĻ350-500 over the window, the family deployed roughly ‚āĻ600-800 Cr of own capital. By Kamayaka's logic, promoters who put ‚āĻ800 Cr into the open market don't believe the stock is going to ‚āĻ200. The strongest read is: family has visibility into FY27-28 US generic launches that the market doesn't see, and at the current 0.89x P/B believes the disconnect closes within 24 months.

But promoter buying is necessary, not sufficient. There's a long list of promoter-bought-back small-mid caps that never operationally recovered (think IL&FS group entities). Buying the same stock the family is buying is reasonable if you can verify their operational thesis. Without concall participation or relationship access, you can't. The market clears at the current discount because the operational case is unverified.

### Deep Dive C ‚ÄĒ The Working Capital Question

Inventory days at 349‚Äď418 on a generic pharma business are extraordinary. Industry median (Ipca, Alembic, JB Chem) sits at 120-180. The plausible explanations:
1. API inventory awaiting customer ANDA-approval pulls (US customers order against ANDA filings; inventory must be ready 6-9 months in advance)
2. EM-market finished-dosage stock awaiting tender awards (lumpy timing)
3. Slow-moving SKUs from legacy India business that haven't been written off

The annual report MD&A would distinguish these. From Screener data alone, only (1) is consistent with the borrowing increase (working capital lent against future revenue) and (2)/(3) would imply impairment risk. The borrowing trend favours (1) but doesn't confirm it.
