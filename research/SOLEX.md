## Summary Verdict

### Why this business?

Solex Energy manufactures solar PV modules (now at 4 GW capacity after rapid expansion) and executes EPC projects across rooftop, C&I, and utility segments. It also does OEM/ODM work for Indian and international brands. The company is moving up the value chain into cell manufacturing (2.2 GW targeted FY27) and has launched a BESS subsidiary. FY26 was a breakout year: revenue more than doubled to ₹1,555 Cr (standalone) with OPM expanding to 11% and PAT reaching ₹89 Cr. Q4 alone delivered ₹839 Cr sales and ₹55 Cr profit — evidence of large order conversion.

The investment case rests on three structural elements that the market is discounting because of SME listing, debt, and one lumpy quarter: (1) domestic solar manufacturing tailwinds (ALMM, BCD, PLI-like support) favor scaled Indian players; (2) vertical integration from modules into cells improves margins and supply security; (3) a multi-thousand crore order book gives visibility while the mainboard migration opens institutional liquidity. ROE of 44% and ROCE of 34% are best-in-class for the small solar/EPC cohort, driven by operating leverage on volume rather than just financial engineering.

The bet is that the cell capex (≈₹1,050 Cr planned) converts into sustained high returns and that debt/EBITDA normalizes as the new capacity ramps. If OPM holds 11%+ and revenue scales toward ₹2,500+ Cr in 2–3 years, the current ~12.6x trailing P/E on a peak year becomes attractive on forward earnings.

### Strengths

- **Scale and capacity already built:** 4 GW module manufacturing capacity (including 2.2 GW n-type TOPCon line commissioned Nov 2025). This is a concrete pre-existing asset — not a plan on paper. It directly supports the jump from sub-₹400 Cr quarterly run-rate to the ₹839 Cr Q4 print.
- **Order book visibility:** >₹3,400 Cr as of Mar 2026 (including EPC). One Q4 n-type TOPCon module order alone was ₹276 Cr. Provides 2+ years of revenue coverage at current scale.
- **Improving operating leverage with scale:** OPM rose from 8% (FY24) to 10% (FY25) to 11% (FY26) even as revenue more than doubled. Unlike some peers, margins expanded rather than contracted on growth.
- **Best-in-class returns:** ROE 44%, ROCE 34% (standalone FY26). Far ahead of direct peer GGBL (ROE ~32%, ROCE ~37% on smaller base). High asset turnover + margin expansion.
- **Policy and listing catalysts:** ALMM enlistment (3.78 GW modules), domestic manufacturing push, and BSE mainboard listing approved (Jun 2026). The latter removes the SME liquidity discount and should attract DII/FII interest. BESS subsidiary (Jun 2026) adds diversification optionality.
- **Strong cash flow recovery:** Operating cash flow turned from -₹49 Cr (FY25) to +₹214 Cr (FY26). FCF positive ₹20 Cr. The Q4 lump helped collections but the swing is real.

### Concerns

- **Leverage and interest burden:** Debt surged from ₹96 Cr (FY24) to ₹324 Cr (FY26) to fund capex. Interest expense ₹30 Cr vs ₹89 Cr PAT. D/E is elevated for a small cap. Cell capex will likely require more debt/equity mix (guided ~₹700 Cr debt for initial 2.2 GW cell line).
- **Revenue lumpiness and normalization risk:** Q4 FY26 was ~54% of full-year sales. Ex-Q4 the business ran closer to ₹700–800 Cr annualized. Need 2–3 clean quarters to confirm sustainable run-rate and utilization on the new capacity.
- **Working capital stretch:** Debtor days rose 47 → 78. Cash conversion cycle tightened overall but receivables on large EPC/utility orders create collection risk. Inventory days also elevated at 81.
- **Capex execution and utilization:** ₹336 Cr fixed assets (from ₹55 Cr) must deliver returns. Cell manufacturing is more capital-intensive and technically demanding than modules. Any delay or low utilization will pressure ROCE and debt metrics.
- **Promoter dilution and historical pledge:** Promoter stake down from ~70.7% to 66.15% via preferential/QIP issuances to fund growth. Some historical promoter pledging noted in older disclosures (monitor fresh filings). Still majority control, but dilution trend is a flag.
- **Policy and competitive volatility:** Solar sector exposed to ALMM changes, import duty shifts, and aggressive pricing from larger players (Waaree etc.). Commodity-like module ASP pressure possible if supply outstrips demand.

High incremental ROIC from operating leverage on module scale + cell integration (reinvestment in capacity at 25-35%+ returns initially) drives 25-35% EPS CAGR for 3 years, then 15%+ as it matures. # Detailed Analysis

## Deep Dives

### A. Cell Manufacturing Economics and Risks

Moving from modules (assembly) to cells (wafer processing + diffusion) is the classic vertical integration play in solar. Module ASPs are more competitive; cells carry higher value-add and technology barriers (PERC → TOPCon → BC). Solex guided ~₹1,050 Cr capex for initial 2.2 GW cell line (phased), funded ~₹700 Cr debt + ₹350 Cr equity/debt mix. Success requires: (1) technology partner (mentions of German ISC Konstanz in filings), (2) high utilization (>70-80% within 18 months), (3) ASP premium over imported cells, and (4) working capital discipline.

If successful, integrated player can protect 2–4 ppt margin vs pure module assemblers and capture more of the value chain. Risk: execution slippage, technology obsolescence, or overcapacity in cells (India adding GW-scale cell lines rapidly). This is the make-or-break 2027–28 event.

### B. Order Book Quality and Lumpiness

>₹3,400 Cr order book (Mar 2026) includes both module supply and full EPC. Q4 execution (₹839 Cr revenue) included at least one ₹276 Cr n-type TOPCon module order from a reputed IPP/group. Large utility/EPC orders are lumpy by nature (milestone billing). The positive: cash flow swung dramatically positive. The watch: receivable aging on these large contracts. If collections lag, working capital will re-absorb the OCF gains.

## 1. Business Summary

Solex Energy Ltd (incorporated 1995, Surat/Gujarat base) is an integrated solar energy player: PV module manufacturing (p-type mono PERC and n-type TOPCon, 4 GW capacity), EPC solutions (residential rooftop to utility), and OEM/ODM supply to Indian/international brands. It has executed 10,000+ projects globally and shipped millions of modules. Recent moves: rapid capacity expansion, push into cell manufacturing (2.2 GW FY27 target), BESS subsidiary (2026), and mainboard migration. Customers span IPPs, C&I, government tenders, and export markets. Policy environment (ALMM, domestic content) is a tailwind for scaled Indian manufacturers.

## Glossary — Key Industry Terms

**ALMM (Approved List of Models and Manufacturers):** MNRE list of approved solar module models/manufacturers. Projects using listed modules get preference or eligibility in certain government tenders — a structural demand tailwind for enlisted Indian capacity like Solex's 3.78 GW.

**TOPCon (Tunnel Oxide Passivated Contact):** Advanced n-type solar cell technology offering higher efficiency than traditional p-type PERC. Solex's newer lines produce TOPCon modules (635–665 W range cited).

**EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction):** Turnkey solar project execution (design, supply modules/inverters, install, commission). Higher margin potential than pure module supply; lumpy revenue recognition.

**BESS (Battery Energy Storage Systems):** Stationary storage paired with solar for time-shifting power. Solex's new subsidiary (Jun 2026) is early diversification beyond pure generation.

**CWIP (Capital Work-in-Progress):** Assets under construction. Low relative to the jump in fixed assets indicates most recent expansion is now commissioned and depreciating.

**Debtor days:** Average collection period for receivables. Rise from 47 to 78 signals larger EPC orders with milestone/credit terms — monitor aging closely.
