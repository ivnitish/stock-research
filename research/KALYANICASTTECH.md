## Summary Verdict

### Why this business?

Kalyani Cast-Tech makes two things: high-quality iron and steel castings (the raw metal components that go into industrial machinery, auto parts, and heavy equipment) and ISO shipping containers. These are two very different businesses sharing a factory floor. The castings business is the higher-quality segment — castings for critical industrial applications require precision metallurgy, quality certifications, and customer-specific tooling, creating modest switching costs. The container business is pure commodity. What's interesting is the growth profile: from ₹63 Cr revenue in FY23 to ₹161 Cr TTM in under 3 years — a near-tripling driven by capacity addition and new customer acquisition. ROCE of 29.6% and debt-free balance sheet by Sep 2025 are genuine quality signals for a manufacturing SME. The bear case is simple: 30x P/E on a commodity-adjacent manufacturer with compressing margins and a promoter reducing stake is expensive. The business needs to prove it can sustain 25%+ revenue growth AND hold OPM above 14% to justify current valuation.

### Strengths

1. **ROCE 29.6%, ROE 24.2% — exceptional for manufacturing.** Very few Indian manufacturing SMEs sustain ROCE above 25%. This suggests real pricing power in at least part of the product mix (likely specialty castings for industrial clients vs commodity containers).
2. **Debt-free by Sep 2025.** Borrowings went from ₹8 Cr (Mar 2024) to ₹0 Cr (Sep 2025) while growing revenue 65%+ — demonstrates strong internal cash generation and disciplined capital management.
3. **Revenue trajectory is genuine.** Sep 2025 half-year (₹92 Cr) up 33% vs Sep 2024 (₹69 Cr) — growth is accelerating, not slowing. If annualised, FY26 revenue could approach ₹180-190 Cr.
4. **Fixed asset expansion.** Fixed assets jumped from ₹8 Cr (Mar 2025) to ₹18 Cr (Sep 2025) — significant capex suggesting management is investing in capacity ahead of demand.

### Concerns

1. **OPM compressing: 17% → 12%.** As revenue scales, margins are falling not expanding — the opposite of what operating leverage should produce. Suggests the new revenue is lower-margin (containers) or input costs are not being passed through. A business with genuinely strong pricing power should show margin expansion at scale.
2. **BSE SME — near-zero liquidity.** Only 2,070 shareholders. Bid-ask spreads will be wide. Any position larger than ₹5-10 lakh will move the price. Hard to exit quickly. Institutional investors cannot participate — no re-rating catalyst from FII/DII buying.
3. **Promoter declining: 69.84% → 59.47% in 2 years.** A 10% reduction in 2 years on a micro-SME with 2,070 shareholders is a yellow flag. Where is the stake going — secondary sales, ESOP dilution, or family restructuring? No explanation found.
4. **30.6x P/E on commodity-adjacent manufacturing.** Even with 29.6% ROCE, a business making castings and containers deserves 18-22x P/E at most given cyclicality, commoditisation risk, and SME liquidity discount. Current valuation leaves no margin of safety.
5. **Single plant concentration risk.** No geographic or facility diversification mentioned. Any operational disruption hits 100% of revenue.
6. **No dividend.** Despite consistent profitability, zero payout — management retaining all cash (possibly appropriate for growth phase, but reduces investor return visibility).

## 1. Business Summary

Kalyani Cast-Tech Ltd (BSE SME: 544023) is incorporated in 2012 and manufactures:
1. **Castings** — iron and steel castings for industrial machinery, automotive components, and heavy engineering. Requires precision metallurgy and customer qualification.
2. **ISO Shipping Containers** — standardised 20ft/40ft containers used in global freight. Pure commodity product competing on price.

The revenue mix between castings and containers is not publicly disclosed — this is a critical gap. If containers are the primary growth driver, the margin compression makes sense (containers are commodity, OPM typically 8-10%) and the ROCE quality deteriorates. If castings are the driver, the business has more durable economics.

**Location:** Single manufacturing facility (location not publicly specified).
**Listing:** BSE SME — migrating to mainboard would be a significant re-rating catalyst (not yet announced).

## 4. Outlook

**FY26E:** Revenue ~₹185-195 Cr (annualising Sep 2025 run-rate of ₹92 Cr per half). PAT ~₹18-20 Cr if OPM holds at 12%. At ₹20 Cr PAT and CMP ₹672, forward P/E = 24x — still not cheap.

**Key question unanswered:** What drove the revenue tripling — new customers, capacity addition, containers vs castings mix? Without this, the growth sustainability is impossible to assess.

**Capex signal:** Fixed assets nearly doubled (₹8 Cr → ₹18 Cr) in 6 months to Sep 2025 — suggests significant capacity addition underway. If this capacity is for higher-margin specialty castings (not containers), margin recovery is possible. If it's container capacity, expect further OPM compression.

**BSE Mainboard migration:** If the company migrates from SME to mainboard (revenue threshold: ₹25+ Cr PAT for 3 years — not yet met), it would be a significant re-rating event. Not imminent.
