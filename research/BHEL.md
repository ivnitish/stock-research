## Why this is a monitor position

BHEL is India's largest PSU power equipment manufacturer — turbines, boilers, generators, and balance-of-plant for thermal, hydro, and nuclear power stations, plus a transmission and industrial systems business. After more than a decade of stagnation while India shifted to renewables and private players ate share, the company is at a possible inflection: India has reverted to ordering large thermal capacity to firm up the grid, and BHEL is the default domestic supplier for super-critical thermal sets. It's worth tracking because the order book has started building again and the PSU is operationally levered to thermal capex — but execution history is poor, working capital is heavy, and the turnaround is unproven.

## What to watch before researching properly

- Quarterly order inflow — is the thermal capex revival showing up in book size?
- Execution rate and working capital days — historically the binding constraint, not order book
- Margin trajectory — can OPM move from low single digits to double digits as scale returns?
