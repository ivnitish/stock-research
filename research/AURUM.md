# Aurum PropTech — Investment Thesis

**Status:** AVOID (0%) — acquisition-led proptech aggregator; returns on capital not yet proven
**Quality Score:** 11/25 (Grade C — real revenue and a fresh profit inflection, but ROIC unproven)
**Classification:** Early turnaround / inflection — not yet a compounder
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-24 | **CMP:** ₹222 | **52w:** ₹151–265 | **Market Cap:** ₹1,601 Cr

> **Recommendation:** AVOID for now — this is a "prove it" story, not a broken one. Aurum PropTech has assembled a suite of real-estate-technology businesses (leasing, rental payments, sales enablement, brokerage tech) largely by acquisition, and FY26 was the first year it stopped losing money: revenue ₹381 Cr (+44%), net profit ₹1 Cr versus a ₹41 Cr loss the prior year. The trailing-twelve-month profit is ₹30 Cr — but the quarterly path (Jun −₹10 Cr, Sep −₹8 Cr, Dec +₹3 Cr, Mar +₹45 Cr) shows almost all of it came in a single quarter, so it is one good print, not an established run-rate. Three-year ROCE is 1.84% and three-year ROE −12% — the capital already deployed (including ₹225 Cr of debt) has not yet earned its keep, and the reported P/E is meaningless (1,704x) on the ₹1 Cr FY26 base. Even on TTM the stock is ~53x on that ₹30 Cr, most of which is one quarter. Until the new profitability proves it can compound at a real return on capital — sustained across quarters, not a single Q4 spike — there is nothing to underwrite here for a concentrated book. Revisit after two-to-three years of ROCE clearing the mid-teens, ideally led by organic operating leverage rather than the next acquisition.

## Why this is an AVOID (not yet, rather than never)

- **Returns on capital have not shown up.** 3-year ROCE 1.84%, 3-year ROE −12%. A proptech roll-up spends real money buying and integrating businesses; the test is whether that capital then earns a high return. So far it has not — the returns are near zero or negative over the measurement window. A single profitable year does not establish the compounding engine the framework requires; it establishes that the losses stopped.
- **The valuation can't be read off trailing earnings.** Reported P/E is 1,704x because FY26 net profit was only ₹1 Cr. Even on TTM profit of ₹30 Cr the stock is ~53x — and that ₹30 Cr is almost entirely one quarter (Mar-2026's ₹45 Cr, against losses in the two quarters before it). So the "cheap on forward earnings" case rests on a single print repeating and growing, which is exactly what an unproven track record can't yet promise.
- **Growth has been bought, not (yet) demonstrably compounded.** The 44% revenue growth and the profit swing sit on top of an acquisitive strategy funded partly by ₹225 Cr of borrowings. Acquisition-led revenue is not the same as organic operating leverage; the framework's mix-shift and ROIC tests need several years of post-integration data that don't exist yet.
- **Book support is thin.** Book value ₹70.4, so at ₹222 the stock is 3.2x book — there is no asset-value floor to fall back on if the profit inflection stalls.

## What's genuinely working (why this is Grade C, not D)

The profit turn is real, not accounting fiction: FY26 operating cash flow was positive (Screener: ₹63 Cr) and operating margins reached 22%, and the Mar-2026 quarter delivered a genuine ₹45 Cr of profit. Revenue is growing fast off a real (₹381 Cr) base in a large, under-digitized Indian real-estate market. This is not Shemaroo — the top line is expanding and the business has started making money. It simply hasn't proven it makes money *consistently*, or *enough* on the capital consumed, to be a compounder — one strong quarter is a hint, not a track record.

## What would change the view
Two-to-three years of ROCE in the mid-teens or better, with the improvement visibly driven by organic operating leverage across the existing platforms (not the next acquisition), and debt trending down as the businesses self-fund. If that prints, re-underwrite as a fresh candidate on forward PEG.

## Growth trigger scan
One partial trigger: an **operating-leverage / turnaround** inflection (losses → profit, margins to 22%). But the framework requires the turnaround to be structural and the returns to follow — here the ROIC proof is missing, so the trigger is a reason to watch, not to buy. No capacity, regulatory, or promoter-buying signal. Promoter holding 47.89% (below the 50% comfort line), FII a negligible 0.42%.
