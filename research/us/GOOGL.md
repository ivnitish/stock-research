## 1. Business Summary

Alphabet is the parent of Google — the world's dominant search engine (90%+ market share), YouTube (2nd largest search), Google Cloud (3rd in cloud behind AWS/Azure and growing), Android (70%+ mobile OS share), and a portfolio of moonshot bets (Waymo, DeepMind).

The core business is **advertising on intent-based search** — when someone searches "best running shoes", Google shows ads to people who are actively looking to buy. This is the highest-quality advertising format ever created. Revenue: $402.8B TTM.

## 5. Valuation

### Why GOOGL at 28x P/E is cheap:

Rule of thumb: P/E should roughly equal growth rate (PEG = 1). GOOGL growing earnings at 31% trading at 28x P/E = PEG of 0.92 = you're getting growth at a discount.

Compare to S&P 500 average P/E ~22x with 8-10% earnings growth. GOOGL has 3x the growth at 1.3x the P/E.

### Scenario Analysis (3 Years — FY28E)

| Scenario | EPS FY28 | P/E Exit | Price | Return from $308.7 |
|----------|---------|---------|-------|---------------------|
| Bear | $17 (15% CAGR) | 18x | $306 | ~0% |
| Base | $22 (25% CAGR) | 22x | $484 | **+57%** |
| Bull | $28 (32% CAGR) | 28x | $784 | **+154%** |

FY25 EPS was ~$10.8 (implied from P/E 28.6 × $308.7). At 25% CAGR to FY28: $10.8 × 1.25³ = $21.1

### What makes this a potential multi-bagger?

At current price, cloud inflection + AI search monetization + buybacks could push EPS significantly above base case. Google has bought back $70B+ shares in FY25, reducing share count ~4-5% annually — this is a permanent EPS tailwind.

**At bull case + buyback effect:** $784 in 3 years = 2.5x. Not a 10x, but in USD terms for a blue-chip, exceptional.

**INR context:** If INR depreciates vs USD (historically 3-4%/year), INR return = USD return + FX gain. ₹50k today → ₹1.27L at base case in 3 years if USD/INR goes to ₹95.
