**Gaming was 90% of revenue in 2019. Data Center is now 85%+.** The business has completely transformed.

### The CUDA Moat Explained
CUDA is like Windows for AI. Millions of AI researchers, engineers, and students have learned CUDA. All major AI frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX) are optimized for CUDA. Switching from NVIDIA to AMD would require:
- Rewriting code (CUDA → ROCm)
- Re-testing all models
- Losing access to NVIDIA's optimized libraries (cuDNN, TensorRT)
- Retraining teams

This is why even at 2x the price, companies keep buying NVIDIA GPUs.

## 5. Valuation

### Scenario Analysis (3-Year, FY28E)

The key variable: does the AI capex cycle sustain, or does hyperscaler spending moderate?

| Scenario | Revenue FY28 | Net Margin | EPS | P/E | Price | vs $186 |
|----------|-------------|-----------|-----|-----|-------|---------|
| Bear (AI pause, competition) | $180B | 45% | $27 | 15x | $405 | +118% |
| Base (sustained AI build-out) | $280B | 52% | $55 | 22x | $1,210 | **+550%** |
| Bull (sovereign AI + new verticals) | $380B | 55% | $79 | 28x | $2,212 | **+1,090%** |

Wait — the bear case still shows 118% upside? Yes, because even if AI pauses, NVIDIA's current $215B revenue at 45% margin generates $27 EPS. At a conservative 15x P/E (mature business), that's $405. The bear case isn't loss; it's just muted upside.

**The genuine bear case:** If AI is oversold and data centers cut back drastically, NVIDIA revenue could drop to $120-150B (from $215B). At $120B, 40% margin, 25x P/E: $120B × 40% / 28.75B shares × 25 = ~$42/share. That's -77%. This is the real bear case — revenue contraction if capex bubble bursts.

### Reverse DCF
At $186 with forward P/E 17.2x: Market expects FY26 EPS ~$10.8 (= $186/17.2). Current TTM EPS = $186/38 = $4.9. FY26 EPS of $10.8 implies just 1 year of 120% growth. Then at 17x P/E, the market is saying NVDA is a "standard" technology company by FY26. This is likely wrong in either direction — too pessimistic about AI demand longevity OR too complacent about competition.
