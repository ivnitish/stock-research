## 1. Business Summary

Amazon is three businesses dressed as one:
1. **AWS (Cloud):** ~$100B ARR, 31% margins — the profit engine
2. **Advertising:** ~$60B revenue, 50%+ margins — the sleeper
3. **Retail (ecommerce + fulfilment):** ~$570B GMV, thin margins — the flywheel anchor

The market price reflects the retail business more than the cloud/ads businesses. This creates the opportunity: you're paying for Amazon at e-commerce margins while getting AWS + Ads for free (almost).

Revenue TTM: $716.9B (largest by revenue among the three US picks).

|---------|-------------------|--------|------------|-----------|
| AWS | ~$105B | 17-20% | ~31% | ~$32B |

| North America Retail | ~$390B | 8-10% | ~6% | ~$23B |
| International Retail | ~$140B | 10%+ | ~2% | ~$3B |
| Subscriptions (Prime) | ~$45B | 12% | ~25% | ~$11B |

**Key insight:** AWS + Ads = ~$165B revenue but generate ~$62B operating profit = **38% of revenue at 38% margin**. The retail business at $530B generates ~$26B = only 5% margin. As AWS and Ads grow faster than retail, Amazon's blended margin will expand significantly. Earnings growth (EPS) will far outpace revenue growth.

This is the "Amazon margin expansion story" — the market has been waiting for it for 10 years and it's now happening (earnings growth 5% sounds slow but look at EPS expansion over 2 years: nearly 3x).
