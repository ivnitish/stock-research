## 4a. Defence/Aerospace Angle — PRIMARY RE-RATING CATALYST

### What the Business Actually Does

Raymond entered aerospace in 2023 via the acquisition of **Maini Precision Products Limited (MPPL)**, which contained an existing aerospace division. This was restructured into **JK Maini Global Aerospace Ltd (JKMGAL)**, a wholly-owned subsidiary (66.3% Raymond-owned, remaining held by Maini family and minority shareholders).

JKMGAL manufactures:
- **Precision aero-engine components** — complex machined parts for jet engines
- Key product: **Nozzle Guide Vanes** for LEAP-1A engines (each aircraft engine contains ₹17–29 Lakh worth of Raymond-made components)
- **Low-pressure turbine components**, fan blades, and other hot-section engine parts
- Approved Tier-1 supplier to GE, Rolls-Royce, Pratt & Whitney, Safran, Airbus, HAL

### Revenue Trajectory

| Quarter | Aerospace & Defence Revenue | YoY Growth | EBITDA | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q3 FY25 | ₹70 Cr | — | ₹14 Cr | 20% |
| Q1 FY26 | ₹87 Cr | +37% | ~₹18 Cr | ~21% |
| Q2 FY26 | ₹81 Cr | +15% | ₹17 Cr | 21% |
| Q3 FY26 | ₹105 Cr | **+48.9%** | ₹19 Cr | 18.1% |

Annualizing Q3 FY26 run-rate: ~₹400–420 Cr/year and accelerating.
Aerospace is ~16% of total revenue today but growing far faster than the auto segment.

### Long-Term OEM Contracts — The Moat

Raymond signed long-term supply agreements with:
- **Pratt & Whitney** (GTF engine family — powers Airbus A320neo, A220)
- **Safran Engines** (CFM56/LEAP — powers Boeing 737 MAX, Airbus A320neo)
- Existing: **GE** (LEAP-1A/1B), **Rolls-Royce**, **HAL**

These are not spot purchase orders. OEM supply agreements in aerospace are typically 5–10 year frameworks with pre-negotiated volumes and pricing. Once certified as a Tier-1 supplier, switching costs are extremely high — OEMs require re-certification (18–24 months, $2–5M cost) to change suppliers for flight-critical parts. This is a durable moat.

### The ₹1,000 Crore Andhra Pradesh Capacity Investment

**Announced October 2025, AP government approved:**
- **JKMGAL** (aerospace): ₹510 Cr investment, 47-acre campus at Gudipalli, Sri Satya Sai District
  - First aerospace-specific plant under Andhra Pradesh's new Aerospace Policy
  - Dedicated low-pressure turbine manufacturing capability
  - 1,400 direct jobs; commercial production target: May 2027
  - AP govt incentives: ~₹380 Cr (land, utilities, capital subsidies)
- **JK Maini Precision Technology** (auto): ₹430 Cr investment, 4,096 jobs

Total: ₹940–1,000 Cr commitment, with capacity coming online by FY28.

This investment effectively doubles the aerospace manufacturing footprint. At 21% EBITDA margins, the incremental revenue from this plant could add ₹200–250 Cr/year EBITDA at maturity (FY28–29).

### Why This Is a Meaningful Catalyst

1. **Revenue growth rate**: 37–49% YoY in a business that is already ₹400 Cr annualized. That is not a rounding error.
2. **Margin profile**: Aerospace earns 21% EBITDA vs 13.9% for auto. As aerospace grows as a share of mix, blended margins expand.
3. **Valuation gap**: MTAR Technologies, Paras Defence, Data Patterns, Azad Engineering all trade at 35–50x EV/EBITDA. Raymond's aerospace business embedded in a holding company at 9.5x is structurally undervalued.
4. **Global supercycle**: Boeing and Airbus have combined backlogs of 12,000+ aircraft (13–14 years of production). Engine OEMs are capacity-constrained. India's China+1 sourcing trend is benefiting Indian precision manufacturers directly.
5. **Institutional recognition**: Nirmal Bang, TradeKaizen, and multiple brokerages have noted the defence/aerospace re-rating angle as of Aug–Sep 2025.

Raymond's aerospace segment is most directly comparable to **Azad Engineering** — both are Tier-1 precision aero-engine component suppliers. Azad trades at 40–43x EV/EBITDA. Raymond's entire engineering company trades at 9.5x. Even if Raymond deserves a discount (mixed portfolio, smaller aerospace share, execution risk on new plant), the gap is extreme.

**Bull case re-rating math:** If JKMGAL alone is valued at 20x EV/EBITDA on ₹420 Cr revenue × 20% margin = ₹84 Cr EBITDA × 20x = ₹1,680 Cr for aerospace alone. Raymond's entire market cap is ~₹2,500 Cr. Auto segment (₹1,600 Cr annualized revenue, 14% margin = ₹224 Cr EBITDA × 8x) = ₹1,792 Cr. Sum-of-parts: ₹3,472 Cr vs current ₹2,500 Cr market cap = 39% upside without growth assumptions.
