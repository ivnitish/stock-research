## 3. The Demerger Confusion

The reported financials are deeply misleading:

| Period | Revenue | Net Profit | What it is |
|--------|---------|-----------|------------|
| FY23 | ₹8,215 Cr | ₹537 Cr | Old Raymond (pre-demerger) |
| FY24 | ₹973 Cr | ₹1,643 Cr | Includes ₹1,719 Cr demerger gains |
| FY25 | ₹1,947 Cr | ₹7,636 Cr | Includes ₹7,741 Cr other income |
| **Real quarterly ops** | ~₹540 Cr | ~₹5-14 Cr | Actual ongoing business |

The "profit" is paper gains from the demerger. Real operational business makes ₹5-14 Cr/quarter on ₹530-560 Cr revenue. That's 1-2.5% net margin — not a quality business.

## 4. The Bull Case (Thane Real Estate)

Raymond's main asset is 100+ acres of prime Thane land. As real estate development progresses:
- FY26-28 revenue recognition from Thane projects could be material
- P/B 0.77x means you're buying the land at a discount to stated book value
- If Thane projects deliver ₹500+ Cr real profit in 3-4 years, current market cap (₹2,518 Cr) looks cheap

**But:** Real estate execution risk is high. Thane land value is real; delivery timelines are uncertain.
