## June 2026 Update — what changed since the March watchlist note

The March-13 note (kept below for reference) had STLTECH at ₹187 with a Grade C "watchlist only" verdict. It correctly identified the three triggers that would matter: DGTR anti-dumping duty on Chinese OFC, two quarters of revenue growth, and a return to positive PAT. All three have now fired — plus a fourth catalyst the note couldn't have anticipated. The result is a 7x rally and a stock now priced for execution it has not yet delivered.

### The four catalysts, ranked by stock-price impact

**1. The $1.11 billion US hyperscaler order (May 2026) — single largest contributor to the rally.**

STL disclosed a multi-year Product Award Letter from a US hyperscale customer worth $1.11 bn (~₹10,000 Cr) for optical connectivity products, delivered through March 2029. The arithmetic: ~$370 M/year × ~₹83/$ ≈ ₹3,000-3,300 Cr/year of additional revenue. STLTECH's TTM revenue is ₹5,750 Cr — so this single order adds **55-60% to revenue** at margins materially better than commodity OFC (export, premium, hyperscale-grade, dollar-billed). At 15-18% OPM, that's ₹500-600 Cr of incremental annual EBITDA against a TTM PAT of just ₹56 Cr. The order doesn't merely lift earnings — it changes what STLTECH is.

**2. DGTR anti-dumping duty on Chinese, Korean, and Indonesian OFC.**

Effective August 2025 (gazette notification Aug 3, 2025), 5-year duration, duty range $122-857 per KFKM. The petitioner was Birla Furukawa — the MP Birla group's optical fiber JV with Furukawa Electric of Japan. The duty restored domestic pricing power and is now visible in the quarterly print: Dec '25 was a -₹17 Cr loss, Mar '26 swung to +₹59 Cr PAT on ₹1,441 Cr revenue. Margin recovery is in motion before the hyperscaler order has even begun contributing.

**3. AI-data-center thematic re-rating.**

The market has stopped pricing STLTECH as "Indian OFC manufacturer hurt by China" and started pricing it as AI-era digital infrastructure. The physical fact behind the label change: AI data centers need 10-36x more optical fiber per MW than traditional cloud DCs because GPU clusters require dense optical interconnects everywhere whereas cloud workloads do not. Global hyperscaler capex is forecast at $1.2T over 2025-2027. This is the same label-shift that moved Vertiv, Coherent, and the US fiber pack 2023-24.

**4. India DC capacity buildout.**

Installed DC capacity moves from 1.4 GW (2025) to 8 GW (2030), with roughly $30 bn of capex flowing through. STLTECH supplies the fiber backbone of that buildout and now has the hyperscaler endorsement to win share globally.

### Lesson from the missed call

The March note got the business analysis right but treated the stock as static. The note's "watch for DGTR + 2 quarters revenue growth + positive PAT" was the right framework — but I treated it as a list of conditions to wait for serially, rather than as a thesis to size into incrementally as each condition fired. By the time all three fired, the market had already moved 4x. The framework lesson: when a watchlist note identifies 2-3 specific catalysts that would make the stock interesting, build a 1-2% tracking position *before* all of them fire. Asymmetric setups don't ring a bell when they turn.
