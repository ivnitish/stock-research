# Shemaroo Entertainment — Investment Thesis

**Status:** AVOID (0%) — loss-making content library with shrinking revenue; no compounding engine
**Quality Score:** 6/25 (Grade D — business quality too poor for price to fix)
**Classification:** Value trap — cheap on book, broken on economics
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-24 | **CMP:** ₹123 | **52w:** ₹73–143 | **Market Cap:** ₹355 Cr

> **Recommendation:** AVOID. Shemaroo trades at 1.3x book and looks statistically cheap, but it is a media company whose revenue fell 15% last year (₹685 Cr → ₹583 Cr) while it posted a ₹218 Cr net loss — larger than the ₹84 Cr loss the year before. Three-year ROCE is −9% and ROE −24%: the business is destroying capital, not compounding it. A low price on book value cannot rescue a content library that is losing money faster each year against ₹302 Cr of debt. There is no price level at which this is a buy for a compounding book; it only becomes interesting after a demonstrated operating turnaround, which is not visible.

## Why this is an AVOID

Shemaroo owns and monetizes a library of Hindi film and devotional content across satellite, digital (ShemarooMe OTT), and broadcast. The problem is structural: its traditional syndication and TV-content revenue is being compressed between the streaming platforms that now commission their own content and the free-content flood on YouTube, while its own OTT push burns cash without reaching the scale of a Netflix or JioHotstar. The financials show the squeeze without ambiguity.

- **Revenue is shrinking, not growing.** FY26 revenue ₹583 Cr, down 15% from ₹685 Cr in FY25. TTM ₹575 Cr. A content business that cannot grow the top line has no runway — the entire multi-bagger math (returns on reinvested capital over a long runway) collapses when both the return and the runway are negative.
- **Deepening losses.** FY26 net loss ₹218 Cr, versus a ₹84 Cr loss in FY25 — the loss more than doubled. TTM loss ₹180 Cr. Operating margin swung to roughly −45% in FY26 from −12% the year before. This is acceleration in the wrong direction.
- **Capital destruction.** 3-year ROCE −9%, ROE −24%. Book value ₹94/share, so the stock at ₹123 is 1.3x book — but book value is being eroded by the losses each year, so the "cheap on book" argument shrinks with every quarter.
- **Debt against a shrinking business.** Borrowings ₹302 Cr with flagged low interest coverage, plus ₹192 Cr of contingent liabilities — meaningful relative to a ₹355 Cr market cap. Losing money while carrying debt is how equity gets wiped, not how it compounds.
- **The one bright spot is not enough.** FY26 operating cash flow was mildly positive (₹24 Cr) despite the ₹218 Cr accounting loss — the loss is partly non-cash (content amortization / write-downs). Debtor days improved to 43. But mildly positive OCF on a shrinking, over-levered, loss-making base is survival, not a thesis.

## Margin of safety

There is no meaningful base-case fair value to anchor to when 3-year ROIC is negative and revenue is falling — a DCF on a declining, loss-making cash stream produces a value well below book, not above it. The 1.3x book multiple is not a margin of safety because book itself is deteriorating. Grade D businesses do not buy at any price (framework Phase 5.2): quality this poor cannot be fixed by a low multiple.

## What would change the view

Two consecutive quarters of (a) revenue stabilizing or growing, AND (b) a return to operating profit with debt coming down — proof the OTT/library model has found a floor. Absent that, this stays AVOID regardless of how cheap it looks on book.

## Growth trigger scan

None of the six firing. No capacity/operating-leverage story (revenue declining), no margin expansion (margins deteriorating), no regulatory tailwind, no promoter-buying signal, and the "turnaround" trigger explicitly fails its test — the pain here is structural (content-value compression), not a temporary, resolvable one-time. Promoter holds 67.2%, which is alignment but not a catalyst.

Shemaroo Entertainment Ltd monetizes a media content library — Hindi films, devotional/regional content, music — across TV syndication, its ShemarooMe OTT app, YouTube, and broadcast/FAST channels.
