Multi-Bagger Pattern Study Queue

Backward-looking studies for learning, not forward-looking buy candidates. Each entry: what kind of pattern it tests, why it's worth the time, source.

The point of this queue is to extend the synthesis in multibagger_patterns.md beyond the trough/disconnect path. The original 5 studies (BSE, BAF, Titan, Page, APL) all caught the stock at extreme pessimism with statistical cheapness present. The candidates below test the opposite path — quality already recognized, market re-rated in real time, trailing valuation high almost the entire compounding phase, but earnings caught up.

✅ Completed studies

  • multibagger_BSE.md — BSE Ltd (2018-19, ~119x). Fortress balance sheet + dormant SME platform + latent derivatives optionality.
  • multibagger_BAF.md — Bajaj Finance (2010-11, ~100x). Consumer NBFC pivot, Rajeev Jain hire, NPA root cause fixed.
  • multibagger_TITAN.md — Titan (2002-03, ~150x). Tanishq segment growth buried under consolidated -55% PAT.
  • multibagger_PAGE.md — Page Industries (2008-09, ~80x). 50-year Jockey license, Genomal family Philippines track record.
  • multibagger_APLAPOLLO.md — APL Apollo Tubes (2014-15, ~30x). #1 organized share + GST equalizer + capex cycle absorbing PAT.

Queued — "Quality re-rates ahead of earnings" pattern

Source: tweet observation 2026-05-10 — 8-example pattern of multi-baggers that "always looked expensive" through their biggest compounding phase. The study target is to identify what was visible BEFORE the headline EPS reflected the future, and to validate or refine the appendix pattern in multibagger_patterns.md.

Astral Limited (formerly Astral Poly Technik)

  • Phase: ~2014-2022, market cap roughly ₹2,000 Cr → ₹40,000+ Cr
  • Hypothesis to test: Distribution-density compounding (continuous dealer addition) + adjacency expansion (ResiQuick adhesives acquisition) + building materials ecosystem build-out, all visible in concalls and investor presentations long before headline revenue (₹700-800 Cr → ₹5,500+ Cr) reflected it. Trailing P/E sat at 40-50x almost the whole period.
  • Specific things to verify: dealer count trajectory year by year; adhesives segment as % of revenue; concall language on building materials adjacency.
  • Why this one matters: Cleanest single example of the pattern — one product (PVC pipes) that the market consistently called "expensive" while the underlying franchise compounded mix and distribution.

KEI Industries

  • Phase: ~2018-2024, revenue ₹3,000-3,200 Cr → ₹9,000+ Cr
  • Hypothesis to test: Retail mix percentage rising sharply against industrial cables base; dealer network expansion; operating margin holding through copper volatility (this is the discriminator vs commodity cable peers). Market refused to pay for a "small cables business firm" at stretched multiples in 2018-19.
  • Specific things to verify: retail revenue as % of total over time; dealer count; OPM trend through copper price swings (LME copper went ₹400/kg → ₹950/kg in this window, then back).
  • Why this one matters: Tests whether the pattern works in a commodity-input business — distinct from Astral's branded-product setup.

Varun Beverages (VBL)

  • Phase: ~2017-2024, revenue ₹5,000 Cr → ₹20,000+ Cr
  • Hypothesis to test: Bottling territory expansion (geographic, both India deeper and overseas) + operating leverage on a fixed-cost-heavy bottling network + PepsiCo distribution rights extension. Market kept calling it "an overvalued bottler" through most of the run.
  • Specific things to verify: territory count year by year; overseas territory share of revenue; volume vs realization split in concall guidance; capex per new bottling line vs incremental EBITDA.
  • Why this one matters: Clearest geographic-expansion-as-distribution-density example. Different physical mechanism from Astral (territory, not dealer count).

Deepak Nitrite

  • Phase: ~2017-2022, PAT ~₹150 Cr → ~₹1,000+ Cr
  • Hypothesis to test: Phenolics greenfield capacity (single-asset transformation) — the inflection isn't gradual mix-shift, it's a single capex project that changed the company's earnings power. PAT moved 6x. ROCE surged. The market read it as "another cyclical chemical" pre-commissioning.
  • Specific things to verify: Phenolics plant commissioning timeline; pre-commissioning sell-side notes vs post-commissioning notes; ROCE before vs after; whether the capex was visible in CWIP for 2-3 years before the inflection.
  • Why this one matters: Tests a different sub-pattern — single-project transformation vs continuous mix-shift. Closer to Phase 0.5's "Built but underutilized" form. The question is what was visible on the balance sheet 2 years before the PAT moved.

Garware Hi-Tech Films

  • Phase: ~2018-2024, mental classification shift from "commodity packaging films" to "specialty manufacturing"
  • Hypothesis to test: Mix-shift from commodity packaging films to specialty (Sun Control films, paint protection films, export-focused high-margin products). The pattern here is mental category change driven by real mix change — same factories, different product mix.
  • Specific things to verify: specialty product revenue as % of total over time; export % of revenue; OPM by segment.
  • Why this one matters: Pure mix-shift example with no major capex story. Tests whether the pattern works without a balance sheet inflection.

Fine Organic Industries

  • Phase: Listed 2018, sustained premium valuation entire post-listing period
  • Hypothesis to test: Niche specialty business (food and polymer additives, export positioning) with structurally high margins (20%+) and ROCE — the market understood the niche from day one and never let the multiple compress to "value" levels.
  • Specific things to verify: OPM trajectory; export % of revenue; customer concentration; reinvestment rate vs ROCE (the Munger-style compounding test).
  • Why this one matters: Tests the limit case — a stock that NEVER traded cheap. If the framework's margin-of-safety discipline would have ruled it out, the framework needs a hook that lets a Fine-Organic-style name through. Or it doesn't, and that's the answer.

Cera Sanitaryware

  • Phase: ~2010-2018 was the cleanest run; recent years more muted
  • Hypothesis to test: Boring compounder via dealer expansion + premium bathroom product mix shift + housing demand tailwind. The "low-effort multibagger" pattern where nothing dramatic happens — just slow, steady distribution density compounding.
  • Specific things to verify: dealer count year by year; premium SKU revenue %; same-store dealer revenue trend.
  • Why this one matters: Counter-example to the dramatic-inflection studies. Tests whether the pattern is identifiable in advance when the slope is gentle.

Order of priority

1. Astral — cleanest single example, validates the core appendix claim

2. Deepak Nitrite — tests the "single capex project" sub-pattern; closest to existing Phase 0.5 framework

3. Varun Beverages — tests geographic-expansion-as-distribution-density

4. KEI Industries — tests the pattern in a commodity-input business

5. Garware / Fine Organic / Cera as time permits — each tests a limit case

3-4 studies should be enough to validate or refine the appendix into a promotable framework section.

What each study should produce (template per multibagger_BAF.md style)

1. The setup — price, multiples, narrative at the entry window

2. What was visible at the time — concall quotes, segment data, dealer/territory/capacity counts, sell-side commentary

3. What the bear case got right and what it got wrong — the symptom-vs-root-cause split

4. The physical mechanism that compounded — specific factory, dealer, territory, mix-shift

5. What you'd need to have believed — to underwrite the position at entry

6. What the framework would have said at the time — does our current framework catch this, miss this, or block this with a margin-of-safety check?

7. Pattern feedback to multibagger_patterns.md — what to add, refine, or contradict
