> **Recommendation:** AVOID for a multi-bagger portfolio. Redington is India's largest IT product distributor — a high-revenue, ultra-thin-margin business that generates real cash but builds no durable competitive advantage. At 12.8x P/E and 3% dividend yield it looks statistically cheap, but the cheapness is deserved: 2% OPM, lumpy OCF, Gulf operations currently restricted due to geopolitical risk, and zero promoter skin in the game. ## Summary Verdict

### Why this business?

Redington is the unglamorous backbone of India's tech economy — the company that actually gets Apple iPhones, HP laptops, Dell servers, and Samsung devices from the manufacturer's factory into 37 countries across India, the Middle East, Turkey, and Africa. Since 1993, they have built a distribution network with three automated warehouses (Chennai, Kolkata, Dubai) and relationships with virtually every global tech OEM. Revenue has compounded at 12% CAGR over 10 years to ₹99,334 Cr in FY25. The problem is that distribution is a scale game, not a quality game. Every rupee of revenue passes through at 2% operating margin. There is no pricing power, no switching cost, no customer lock-in — a manufacturer can change distributors with 90-day notice. What Redington has is size, logistics infrastructure, and working capital efficiency. What it doesn't have is a moat. The stock is cheap because it should be cheap.

### Strengths

1. **Scale and vendor relationships.** At ₹99,334 Cr revenue, Redington is simply too big to displace easily. OEMs prefer large distributors — fewer partners to manage, guaranteed volume, consistent payments. This is not a moat but it is a defensible position.
2. **Geography diversification.** India, Middle East, Turkey, Africa — unlike pure India IT distributors, Redington has 37-country reach. This creates resilience when any single market slows.
3. **12.8x P/E and 3% dividend yield.** At ₹229, the market is pricing in zero growth. If revenue just compounds at 10% and margins hold, fair value is ₹260-280 on current earnings. Not expensive on a DCF.
4. **ROCE 18.9%.** Surprisingly decent for a thin-margin distributor — reflects asset-light model (OEMs hold inventory risk, Redington takes working capital risk) and efficient capital deployment.
5. **Institutional confidence: FII 61.49%, DII 17.12%.** 78% institutional ownership signals this is a well-understood business, not a hidden gem — but not a value trap either.

### Concerns

1. **2% OPM — no margin of safety.** Any revenue disruption, vendor rebate change, or working capital blowout goes straight through to profit. FY23 OCF was -₹3,234 Cr (negative), FY25 was +₹293 Cr. That swing from +₹3,497 Cr (FY21) to -₹3,234 Cr (FY23) in two years illustrates the risk.
2. **Gulf operations restricted (Apr 2026).** "Redington Gulf FZE operations restricted due to geopolitical developments" — unquantified. Middle East is a large geography. If the restriction is prolonged, it could materially hit FY27 revenue and cash flow. No timeline given.
3. **FY25 PAT of ₹1,821 Cr is inflated.** Other income was ₹854 Cr in FY25 vs ₹264 Cr in FY24 — a ₹590 Cr jump that is unlikely recurring. Normalized core PAT: ~₹1,200-1,300 Cr → actual P/E closer to 14-15x.
4. **Zero promoter holding.** Redington's Singapore-listed parent (SCI Systems) restructured, leaving no identifiable promoter. While not a governance red flag given 78% institutional ownership, there is no founding-family alignment with minority shareholders.
5. **CCC expanding: 14 days (FY21) → 36 days (FY25).** Cash conversion cycle nearly tripling means Redington is financing more working capital. This is not a moat signal — it means customers are paying slower and/or vendors are tightening credit terms.
6. **No compounding engine.** EPS has grown from ₹5.30 (FY16) to ₹20.53 (FY25) — a 16% CAGR on paper, but this includes FY25's inflated other income. Normalized EPS CAGR is closer to 10-11%. A distributor compounding at 10% EPS with 2% OPM does not become a multi-bagger.

## 1. Business Summary

Redington Ltd (NSE: REDINGTON) is India's largest IT product distributor, incorporated in 1993. The company acts as a middleman between global tech manufacturers (Apple, HP, Dell, Samsung, Lenovo, Cisco, etc.) and end markets across 37 countries.

**Three business lines:**
1. **IT Products** — laptops, desktops, servers, networking, storage (core business, ~70% revenue)
2. **Mobility** — smartphones, tablets, accessories (~25% revenue)
3. **ProConnect / Supply Chain Services** — value-added logistics, cloud distribution, managed services (emerging, ~5% revenue — the quality business)

**Geographic split (approximate):**
- India: ~45% of revenue
- Middle East + Turkey + Africa (via Redington Gulf FZE): ~55% of revenue

**Key OEM relationships:** Apple, HP, Dell, Samsung, Lenovo, Cisco, Microsoft, Sony — top 5 OEMs likely >70% of revenue. No public concentration breakup available.

## 4. Outlook

**Near-term headwinds:**
- Gulf operations restricted — Middle East is ~55% of revenue. Even a partial disruption for 2-3 quarters materially impacts FY27 numbers. No resolution timeline public.
- OCF improvement needed — ₹293 Cr OCF on ₹99,334 Cr revenue (0.3% OCF yield) is very thin. Working capital normalisation is the key lever.

**Medium-term opportunities:**
- ProConnect (supply chain + cloud services) — if this scales to 10%+ of revenue, the margin profile improves meaningfully. This is the only business where Redington could build differentiation.
- India data centre build-out (2025-2030) — hyperscaler investment in India drives enterprise IT equipment demand that Redington distributes.
- Smartphone upgrade cycle — India's premium smartphone market (>₹30,000 price point) growing at 20%+ is margin-accretive for Redington vs low-end devices.

---

## 5. Valuation

At ₹229 and normalized PAT ~₹1,250 Cr: **P/E ~14-15x**

For a no-moat IT distributor with Gulf risk, 14-15x normalized P/E is approximately fair. The dividend yield of 3% provides a floor. Upside requires either:
- OPM expansion (from 2% toward 3%) — requires services pivot
- Gulf resolution + OCF normalization — binary event risk

**Entry only if**: below ₹180 (10-11x normalized earnings) where yield exceeds 4%.
