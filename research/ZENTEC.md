## Why this is a monitor position

Zen Technologies makes combat training simulators, anti-drone systems, and AI-driven defence training solutions for the Indian armed forces and paramilitary. The thesis is straightforward in shape — India's defence indigenisation push (iDEX, positive indigenisation lists, capital allocation toward made-in-India systems) gives a domestic simulator specialist a multi-year tailwind, and the company has been adding capability through acquisitions (AI Turing, Applied Research International) and capacity build-out. It's interesting enough to track because the order book trajectory and operating leverage on a small revenue base could be material if defence capex flows through as guided.

## What to watch before researching properly

- Quarterly order inflow vs execution — is the book-to-bill ratio sustaining above 1.0x?
- Margin trajectory as new product categories (anti-drone, AI training) scale — does mix shift help OPM?
- Acquired entity integration — are AI Turing and ARI contributing visible revenue and margin?
- Defence capex cycle — Union Budget allocation and MoD order announcements
