## What each is really betting on

### NEETUYOSHI: a foundry with a lifetime tax break, capex done, guidance explicit

The Yoshi bet is that a specific set of already-visible things convert to numbers in the next 4-6 quarters:
- The new plant that just started producing in Jun 2026 fills up to the ₹200 Cr revenue ceiling
- RDSO approval for that new plant comes in July 2026 (the direct-Railway supply switch)
- The old plant continues at ₹110 Cr
- Track business grows from ₹20 Cr to ₹60-70 Cr over two years
- Total FY28: ~₹350 Cr revenue, ₹85-90 Cr PAT at 25% margin (protected by 17.5% tax rate + Railway PVC pass-through)

Everything above requires execution but the physical asset is built, the certifications are 27 deep with 15-20 in pipeline, and the tax structure is a permanent 7.5pp margin advantage over 25%-taxed peers. The bet is: "will management deliver on numbers they've publicly committed to?"

### KALYANICASTTECH: a vision worth 10x if the whole ecosystem is built

The Kalyani bet is much larger and more speculative. The promoter — a 17-year IIT Madras + Class A Indian Railways officer who personally designed India's double-stack container train system — is trying to build a private-sector version of what Braithwaite (PSU) does but never scaled: containers + wagons + terminals + foundry, all vertically integrated at one 144-acre Gujarat site.

The immediate business (specialized containers) is real and growing: 85% utilization on 6,000 units, expanding to 16,000 units by Mar 2026 = 2.67x capacity. Container-only revenue on 80% utilization of the new capacity is already ₹300+ Cr. That's the floor.

The upside is wagons (Phase 1 = 2,500 wagons/year, target 7,500-7,800) and the Gati Shakti terminal (a permanent logistics-handling fee stream). But wagons need G-105 certification (still not filed), Phase 1 revenue is H2 FY27 at earliest, and the ₹4,000 Cr vision requires all four segments delivering.

The bet is: "will the promoter build the ecosystem he says he can build?" That's a much longer, more optionality-heavy bet than Yoshi's "will he deliver the ₹210-220 Cr FY27 guidance?"

## Where each is stronger

### Yoshi is materially stronger on

- **Margins.** 25% PAT margin (guided) vs 10-12% at Kalyani. This is not a small difference — it means Yoshi's earnings on the same revenue are ~2.3× Kalyani's. This is the biggest single fact when comparing the two.
- **Tax advantage.** 17.5% lifetime rate vs 25% — a permanent 10% net-margin edge. Compounded over 10 years, this is massive.
- **Capex complete.** Yoshi's Kanpur plant is commissioned and producing. Kalyani's wagon plant is still being built with certification pending.
- **Guidance precision.** Yoshi gives ₹210-220 Cr revenue and 25% PAT margin — testable in 4 quarters. Kalyani gives a ₹4,000 Cr vision — testable in 7 years.
- **Promoter stability.** 70% flat vs 60% and drifting.
- **Cash conversion.** Yoshi's Feb-Mar receivables spike is now understood as Railway budget-cycle seasonality; Kalyani's structural OPM 10-12% means CFO is always going to be thin.

### Kalyani is materially stronger on

- **TAM ceiling.** ₹4,000 Cr vision vs Yoshi's ₹350 Cr FY28 target and ~₹1,000 Cr 10x-case ceiling. If Kalyani executes, it becomes a materially larger business than Yoshi ever will.
- **Vertical integration.** Containers + wagons + terminal + foundry at one site is a moat that Yoshi cannot replicate — Yoshi is a foundry supplier to the ecosystem, Kalyani wants to BE the ecosystem.
- **Promoter domain depth.** Naresh Kumar personally designed India's double-stack container train system while in Railways. Yoshi's promoter (Himanshu Lohia) is a competent manufacturing operator without that specific Railways insider background.
- **RDSO exclusivity.** Kalyani co-designed the specialized wagon *with* RDSO, giving exclusive manufacturing rights. Yoshi has approvals for 27 products but shares the market with 4-5 to 7 other qualified suppliers per product.
- **Integrated logistics optionality.** Gati Shakti terminal creates permanent handling-fee revenue independent of what Kalyani manufactures. Yoshi has no analogous stream.

## Where each has to be watched

### Yoshi risks

- Track business scaling is working-capital intensive; ₹29 Cr warrants raise covers it but doesn't leave slack
- FY27 guidance requires the new plant to ramp on schedule; slip means H2-heavy revenue with H1 disappointment
- Bogie pricing softness (₹3.25 lakh → ₹2.70-2.80 lakh) is a real headwind until wagon cycle recovers
- Customer concentration on wagon/rail ecosystem (Titagarh, Jupiter mentioned) — not diversified beyond Railway sales channel
- BSE SME liquidity

### Kalyani risks

- G-105 certification not filed yet — every wagon-revenue timeline slips if this slips
- Promoter dilution 69.8% → 59.5% unresolved (management "refused further discussion")
- ₹170-200 Cr capex on ₹461 Cr MCap = 40% of MCap. If wagon revenue delayed 2-3 years, interest burden compresses earnings
- 10-12% OPM structural — a manufacturing business that will never look like a compounder even if revenue triples
- Execution complexity: three parallel initiatives (containers, wagons, terminal) any of which can slip
- BSE SME liquidity

## Portfolio construction — how to think about owning both

These are complementary bets, not overlapping ones:

- **Yoshi is a "guidance delivery" bet with a 4-quarter horizon.** Hard catalyst (RDSO new-plant approval July 2026), explicit numeric guidance, permanent tax moat. Position sized at 5-7% initial, add to 15% core on delivery.
- **Kalyani is a "vision execution" bet with a 3-5 year horizon.** No hard near-term catalyst until G-105 filing, but ₹4,000 Cr TAM if the ecosystem is built. Position sized much smaller — max 3-4% and only at the right entry (below ₹450 per the current research file).

Owning both means: capturing the near-term railway-manufacturing re-rating cleanly through Yoshi + holding a longer-dated, higher-upside call option on the vertically-integrated logistics vision through a smaller Kalyani position.
